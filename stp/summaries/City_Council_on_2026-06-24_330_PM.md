#### Meeting Info
**Type:** City Council Meeting
**Purpose:** Regular City Council Session & Public Hearings
**Date/Time:** June 24, 2026, at 3:30 PM
**Context:** The meeting included several proclamations for community recognition and a significant discussion regarding tree preservation.

#### Summary
The following items were discussed and voted upon during the meeting. All votes recorded in this session were unanimous (6-0).

*   **Community Recognitions & Proclamations:**
    *   **Opportunity Zones:** Resolution 26-1011 was adopted to support recommendations for selecting census tracts as opportunity zones. This was pulled from the consent agenda briefly due to a technical update before being approved.
    *   **Dr. Kalbia Vang Memorial Days:** The Council officially recognized the life of Lieutenant Colonel Dr. Kalbia Vang, a long-time community leader and dentist. July 11–13, 2026, is proclaimed as "Lieutenant Colonel Dr. Ku B. Vang Memorial Days."
    *   **Day of Compassion:** Resolution 26-1014 was adopted to recognize the Tibetan community in St. Paul and designate July 6, 2026, as the "Day of Compassion" for the 91st birthday of the Dalai Lama.
    *   **Pride Month:** The Council officially recognized June 2026 as Pride Month. The resolution includes a commitment to ensure city departments provide inclusive facilities (such as gender-inclusive restrooms) and maintain supportive workplace practices for LGBTQIA2S Plus employees.

*   **Public Works & Infrastructure:**
    *   **Tree Preservation Ordinance:** A significant new ordinance (205-4) regarding tree preservation during city-sponsored construction projects was discussed. The plan aims to protect the urban canopy, establish stricter rules for "tree protection zones," and create a system of penalties/restitution for contractors who cause damage to trees. This item is **scheduled for a public hearing on July 8th** following further deliberation.
    *   **Drain Management:** A discussion was held regarding the "Adopt-A-Drain" program to promote watershed health and prevent flooding through community engagement with storm drains.

*   **Public Hearings & Safety:**
    *   **Sound Variances:** The Council approved several sound level variances for:
        *   District Energy boiler testing (June 29 – July 17).
        *   The Rondo Days Festival on July 18, 2026.
    *   **Police Department Funding:** Resolution 206-142 was adopted to allow the police department to adjust and manage their spending plans within their allotted grant funds.
