#### Meeting Info
**Type:** City Council Meeting  
**Purpose:** Consideration of various resolutions regarding community recognition, infrastructure protection, and public safety measures.  
**Date/Time:** June 24, 2026, at 3:30 PM.

#### Summary
The meeting included several unanimous votes (6-0) on a variety of topics ranging from local community honors to infrastructure and public safety.

*   **Community Recognition & Honors:**
    *   **Memorial for Lt. Col. Dr. Ku B. Vang:** The Council approved a resolution to proclaim July 11–13, 2026, as "Lieutenant Colonel Dr. Ku B. Vang Memorial Days." The council recognized his significant contributions to the Hmong community and his service in the Air National Guard.
    *   **Day of Compassion:** Resolution 26-1014 was passed to recognize July 6, 2026, as "Day of Compassion" in honor of the Dalai Lama’s 91st birthday and to acknowledge the contributions of St. Paul's Tibetan community.
    *   **Pride Month:** The Council officially recognized June 2026 as Pride Month, reaffirming their commitment to inclusiveer policies and safety for LGBTQIA2S Plus residents.

*   **Infrastructure & Environment (Tree Preservation):**
    *   The council reviewed a significant ordinance regarding **tree preservation during city-sponsored construction**. This was the result of an 18-month collaborative effort between Parks and Public Works. The ordinance establishes standards for tree inventories, protection zones, and requires contractors to be financially accountable for damage. 
    *   **Note:** While the council expressed strong support for the direction of this policy, it was **laid over until July 8th** for a second reading/public hearing. Discussions touched on the cost of hiring arbors and the need for long-term sustainability in staffing the forestry units.

*   **Public Safety & Quality of Life:**
    *   **Police Budget:** A resolution was passed to allow the police department to adjust funding and spending plans within their grant funds. 
    *   **Sound Variances:** The Council approved two sound level variances: one for a District Energy boiler testing event (June 29 – July 17) and another for the Rondo Days Festival on July 18, 2026.
    *   **Water Quality/Drainage:** A resolution was passed regarding "Adopt-A-Drain" initiatives to promote community involvement in keeping storm drains clear to protect the watershed and prevent flooding.

*   **Economic Development:**
    *   The Council approved a resolution (Resolution 26-1011) regarding the ranking of eligible census tracts for designation as **opportunity zones**.
