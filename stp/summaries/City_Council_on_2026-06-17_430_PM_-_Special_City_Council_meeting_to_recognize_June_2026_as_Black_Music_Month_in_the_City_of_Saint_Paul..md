#### Meeting Info
**Type:** Special City Council meeting
**Purpose:** Resolution 26-996 - To recognize June 2026 as Black Music Month in the City of Saint Paul and to honor local artists and organizations for their cultural contributions.
**Date/Time:** June 17, 2026, at 4:30 PM

#### Summary
The primary purpose of this meeting was the formal adoption of Resolution 26-996. This resolution officially designates June as "Black Music Month" to celebrate and honor the extensive impact that Black musicians, producers, and educators have had on the culture and history of Saint Paul.

**Key Highlights & Programs:**
*   **The Get Down Program:** The Council heard from representatives of the Parks and Recreation Department regarding "The Get Down." This program provides mentorship for young people (ages 14–24) to find their voice through hip-hop and other musical genres. It focuses on creating community, building confidence, and providing professional opportunities for youth to avoid falling into less productive paths.
*   **Community Impact:** There was a strong emphasis on the role of music as a "universal language" and its power to build diverse communities and provide safe spaces for young people.

**Award Recognitions:**
The Council awarded several local figures and organizations who were identified as key contributors to Saint Paul’s cultural vitality:
*   **Next Verse Award (Emerging Artist):** Yevra Brantley.
*   **Vibe Alchemist Award (Producer/DJ/Artist):** DJ Mickey Breeze.
*   **Stage Builder Award (Organizer/Promoter):** Trini C. Productions (Marina).
*   **Community Harmony Award (Advocacy/Broadcasting):** Georgia Ford and the team at Power 104.7 FM.
*   **651 Sound Award (Local Talent):** Alan Kingdom.
*   **Rhythm and Roots Award (Education/Preservation):** Elder Mahmoud El-Khati.
*   **Living Legacy Award (Long-term Community Impact):** David T.C. Ellis, founder of the High School for Recording Arts.

**Voting Results:**
The resolution was passed unanimously by all members present. There were no contested points or debates during this special session; the council expressed broad support for both the specific honorees and the continued investment in youth music programs.
