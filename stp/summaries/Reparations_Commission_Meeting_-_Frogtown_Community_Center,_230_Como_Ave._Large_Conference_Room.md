#### Summary
The meeting began with an introduction by the Chairperson, who explained the purpose of the Reparations Commission and its goals. The commission is tasked with developing a comprehensive reparations plan to address the historical injustices faced by communities of color in Saint Paul.

Key points from the meeting:

* A working group was established to develop a framework for reparative justice.
* The group will focus on three main areas:
	+ Housing: addressing housing disparities and promoting affordable housing options.
	+ Education: improving educational outcomes for students of color, including increasing access to early childhood education and mentorship programs.
	+ Economic Development: creating economic opportunities for communities of color through business development and job training initiatives.
* The commission discussed potential budget allocations for the reparations plan, with a proposed allocation of $10 million over five years.

The meeting was marked by some disagreement between commissioners regarding the scope and pace of the reparative justice efforts. A contentious point of discussion was a proposed amendment to include a provision for restitution to individuals who were wrongfully convicted and imprisoned in the past. While some commissioners supported this measure, others argued that it would be too costly and divert resources from broader reparations efforts. The amendment ultimately failed with 5 votes against and 3 in favor.

Overall, the meeting highlighted ongoing tensions within the commission regarding the pace and scope of reparative justice efforts. However, the establishment of a working group to develop a comprehensive framework for reparations provides a positive step forward.
