#### Meeting Info
**Type:** City Council Regular & Special Meetings
**Purpose:** Legislative actions, community proclamations, and special recognition for local arts and culture.
**Date/Time:** June 17, 2026, at 3:30 PM

#### Summary
The council met to address several items ranging from community celebrations and cultural recognitions to land use, business developments, and infrastructure concerns.

**Community & Cultural Proclamations**
*   **Juneteenth (Resolution 26-997):** The Council officially recognized Juneteenth as a day of celebration for Black freedom and resilience. This included an official proclamation from the Mayor and recognition of several local organizations, such as 30,000 Feet and the Miss Juneteenth Minnesota State pageant.
*   **Black Music Month (Resolution 26-996):** During a special meeting session, the council recognized June 2026 as Black Music Month. They presented a variety of awards to local artists and organizations, including the Next Verse Award, Vibe Alchemist Award, Stage Builder Award, Community Harmony Award, 651 Sound Award, Rhythm and Roots Award, and the Living Legacy Award for the High School for Recording Arts (HSRA).

**Business & Land Use Development**
*   **Cook St. Paul LLC (Resolution 26-1014):** The council approved a change in the scope of work using Neighborhood Star Grant funds to assist the business with major construction on their restaurant.
*   **Zoning Change (Ordinance 26-28):** Property at 213 Bates Avenue was successfully rezoned from H2 Residential to T1 Traditional Neighborhood.
*   **Subdivisions:** Approval was granted for a subdivision by Registered Land Survey for Highland Bridge Property Owner LLC at 2200 Ford Parkway, including the final plat for "Highland Bridge Row Homes."

**Public Safety & Quality of Life**
*   **Sanitary Sewer Lines (Item 30):** A resident provided testimony regarding significant costs (approximately $10,000) incurred due to tree roots growing into a sewer line. While the council noted that existing assessment policies would not change immediately as a result of this specific case, they highlighted that these concerns will be integrated into upcoming discussions regarding new tree planting and preservation ordinances next week.
*   **Event Noise Variances:** The council approved several sound-level variances for events at Harriet Island Park (specifically for the Minnesota Country Club and Minnesota Yacht Club) during mid-July.

**Garbage & Utility Services**
*   **Delinquent Bills:** The council approved two legislative items regarding the assessment of costs for collecting delinquent garbage bills from October to December 2025, with specific properties being referred back to a future hearing for further review.
