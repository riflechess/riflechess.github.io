#### Meeting Info
**Type:** City Council Meeting & Special Meeting
**Purpose:** Regular business meeting including Juneteenth recognitions, land use approvals, and a special session for Black Music Month honors.
**Date/Time:** June 17, 2026 | 3:30 PM

#### Summary
The council took several actions regarding community celebrations and local recognition. They unanimously approved resolutions to honor **Juneteenth** as an annual celebration of black freedom and resilience, which included a special proclamation from the Mayor for "Miss Juneteenth State Pageant Day." Additionally, during a special meeting, the council officially recognized June 2026 as **Black Music Month**, honoring several local artists and organizations.

In terms of business and land use:
*   **Cook St. Paul LLC:** The council fast-tracked a resolution to allow for a change in the scope of work using Neighborhood Star Grant funds, ensuring the owner could begin construction before traveling out of the country.
*   **Zoning & Development:** Property at 213 Bates Avenue was rezoned from H2 Residential to T1 Traditional Neighborhood. The council also approved a subdivision for Highland Bridge Property Owner LLC and granted sound-level variances for events at Harriet Island Park in July.

**Public Safety and Quality of Life Concerns:**
A significant point of discussion arose during a public hearing regarding the **repair of sanitary sewer lines**. A resident reported that a city-planted tree grew into their sewer line, causing $10,000 in damages and resulting in an expensive loan for repairs. While the council stated that current assessment policies would not change immediately due to this specific testimony, they acknowledged the concern regarding where trees are planted over infrastructure and noted it will be shared with staff during upcoming discussions on tree-planting ordinances.

The council also approved several administrative items, including assessments for the collection of delinquent garbage bills for the fall 2025 period.
