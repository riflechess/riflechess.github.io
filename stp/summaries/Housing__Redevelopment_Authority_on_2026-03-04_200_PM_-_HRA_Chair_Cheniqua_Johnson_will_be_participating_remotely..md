#### Meeting Info
* **Meeting Type:** Housing and Redevelopment Authority (HRA) meeting
* **Date/Time:** 2026-03-04, 2:00 PM
* **Attendees:** HRA Chair Cheniqua Johnson participated remotely, along with Council Members and staff members

#### Summary
The Housing and Redevelopment Authority met to discuss updates to the commercial corridor program guidelines. The changes aim to make the program more responsive to community needs while maintaining equity considerations.

Key points from the meeting:

* **Commercial Corridor Program Guidelines Update:** Staff presented an updated version of the program's guidelines, addressing concerns raised by Council Members during a previous meeting. The new guidelines prioritize flexibility for staff to approve projects on a case-by-case basis.
* **Index Score Explanation:** The index score is used to determine award sizes, taking into account factors such as density of businesses, employees, and vacant buildings. The score ranges from 0 to 100, with higher scores indicating greater opportunity for commercial vitality.
* **Subgrants for Operational Expenses:** Staff recommended updating the guidelines to allow for subgrants to support operational expenses in cases where there is a significant disruption along a corridor.

Regarding the Downtown Vitality Fund:

* **Downtown Vitality Fund Overview:** The fund aims to provide financial assistance for both commercial and residential development projects that strengthen and activate downtown St. Paul.
* **Eligible Uses:** The fund will cover building capital improvements, equipment purchases, public room improvements, and other eligible uses.
* **Economic Development Component:** $2 million of the $5 million fund will be allocated for economic development initiatives.

Council Members discussed concerns about the eligibility threshold for businesses to access the commercial corridor funding. Commissioner Yang expressed interest in exploring whether the commercial corridor dollars should be used downtown as well.

The meeting concluded with a discussion on the need for clear communication and potential adjustments to the guidelines. The HRA Chair indicated that she is amenable to adjusting the timeline for finalizing the guidelines if needed.
