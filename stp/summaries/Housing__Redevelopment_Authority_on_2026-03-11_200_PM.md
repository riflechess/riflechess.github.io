#### Meeting Info
* **Type:** Housing and Redevelopment Authority (HRA) meeting
* **Purpose:** To discuss updates on various HRA programs, including commercial corridors, downtown vitality fund, emergency rental assistance, and others
* **Date/Time:** March 11, 2026 at 2:00 PM
* **Relevant Context:** The meeting is part of a series to update the guidelines for various HRA programs.

#### Summary

The meeting focused on updates and discussions regarding various HRA programs. Key points include:

1. Commercial Corridors Program:
	* Discussion around scenarios for allocating funds, including options that would remove downtown from the program.
	* Commissioner Noecker proposed a motion to lay over the item for two weeks to allow more time for consideration.
	* The motion was supported by several commissioners and passed with a roll-call vote (7-0).
2. Resolution Res 26-407: Amending Down Payment Assistance Program Guidelines
	* Discussion around eligibility guidelines for descendants of property owners in Rondo who lost property due to I-94 construction.
	* Commissioner Bowie proposed a motion to lay over the item for two weeks pending staff report on suggested timeline for discussion.
	* The motion was supported by several commissioners and passed with a roll-call vote (7-0).
3. Resolution Res 26-408: Amending Emergency Rental Assistance Eviction Prevention Program Guidelines
	* No discussion or opposition; the resolution was approved with a roll-call vote (7-0).
4. Resolution Res 26-409: Approving Updated Guidelines for HRA Business Assistance Fund (Downtown Vitality Fund)
	* Discussion around non-profit eligibility, with some commissioners expressing concerns about excluding non-profits from funding.
	* Commissioner Noecker proposed an amendment to include development corporations specifically and exclude government institutions and schools.
	* The amendment was accepted as a friendly amendment, and the resolution will be laid over for two weeks pending further language preparation.

Other notable points:

* Director McMahon acknowledged that the staff report was not reviewed by some commissioners before being presented to the board.
* Commissioner Noecker emphasized the importance of having clear guidelines and language prepared in advance to avoid unnecessary delays.
* The meeting concluded with a brief presentation on the Familiar Families Pilot program, which aims to provide wraparound services for families experiencing homelessness.
