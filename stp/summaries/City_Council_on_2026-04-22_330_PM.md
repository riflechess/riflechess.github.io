#### Meeting Info
* Type: City Council Meeting
* Date/Time: 2026-04-22, 3:30 PM
* Purpose: Regular Meeting of the Saint Paul City Council
* Attendees: Members of the City Council, Mayor Kali Hur, staff members, and community representatives

#### Summary

**Appointment of Directors**

The meeting began with the approval of three appointments to city director positions. Deb Barber was appointed as Director of Public Works, Joe Harney as Office of Financial Services Director, and Kevin Young as Communications Manager Assistant to the Mayor.

**Honoring Yusuf M. Genie's Legacy**

Council Member Bowie led a heartfelt tribute to Yusuf M. Genie, a community leader who passed away on April 7, 2026. The council adopted Resolution 26-672, which honored his legacy and contributions to the city of Saint Paul. Several speakers, including family members and community leaders, shared stories about Genie's impact on their lives and the community.

**Resolution Supporting Divestment from ICE**

Council Member Kim introduced a resolution (Resolution 26-619) urging European financial institutions to divest from companies that enable U.S. Department of Homeland Security and Immigration and Customs Enforcement (DHS/ICE). The resolution was laid over to May 6th after some council members expressed concerns about the delegation representing the Twin Cities and its potential impact.

**Ordinance Related to Civil Immigration Laws**

Council Member Coleman introduced Ordinance 26-19, which aimed to strengthen the city's relationship with civil immigration laws and enforcement practices. After several speakers shared their support for the ordinance, the council adopted it.

**Public Hearings and Resolutions**

The meeting included public hearings on Resolution 26-80 (donation from St. Paul Parks Conservancy), Resolution 26-90 (sound level variance for XL Energy), Resolution 26-94 (amending financing and spending plans in the fire department), and Resolution 26-95 (reimbursement from Minnesota Professional Firefighters Association).

**Legislative Hearing Consent Agenda**

The council adopted the legislative hearing consent agenda items 38 through 53 without objection.

**Ward News**

Council Members shared updates on various events and initiatives happening in their wards, including Earth Day celebrations, cleanups, and community events.
