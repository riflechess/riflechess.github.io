#### Meeting Info
* **Meeting Type:** City Council
* **Date/Time:** April 22, 2026, 3:30 PM
* **Purpose:** To discuss and vote on various resolutions and ordinances, including honoring the legacy of Yusuf M. Genie, urging European financial institutions to divest from companies that enable DHS and ICE, and amending city policies related to civil immigration enforcement.
* **Location:** St. Paul City Council Chambers

#### Summary
The meeting began with a resolution (26-662) approving the mayor's appointment of Deb Barber as Director of Public Works, which was passed unanimously.

Next, the council honored the legacy of Yusuf M. Genie, a community leader and civil rights activist who had passed away on April 7, 2026. The resolution, which was read aloud by Council Member Bowie, highlighted Genie's contributions to the city and his commitment to truth, community, and justice.

The council then discussed a resolution (26-619) urging European financial institutions to divest from companies that enable DHS and ICE, but due to concerns raised by Council Members Johnson and Bowie about the lack of information and transparency surrounding the delegation of residents advocating for this divestment, the motion was laid over until May 6th.

The council also discussed several ordinances, including one (26-25) amending Chapter 66 of the Legislative Code to correct a Scrivener's error related to allowing additional lot coverage for providing functional green roof space, which will be laid over until May 6th for second reading and public hearing. Another ordinance (26-26) updating Chapter 310 of the Legislative Code pertaining to uniform license procedures to extend payment of license renewal fees owed in calendar year 2026 was also discussed.

The council held a public hearing on an ordinance (26-19) adding Chapter 44A to the Administrative Code to institute training, reporting, and programming related to the city's relationship with civil immigration laws and enforcement practices. Several community members spoke in favor of the ordinance, citing the need for greater transparency and accountability in the city's handling of immigration-related issues.

The council also approved several resolutions related to financial matters, including one (26-80) authorizing the Department of Public Works to accept a donation from the St. Paul Parks Conservancy and another (26-90) approving an application for a sound level variance for infrastructure work on the CP railroad crossing.

Finally, the council heard reports from its members on various events and initiatives taking place in their wards, including cleanups, art exhibitions, and cultural performances.
