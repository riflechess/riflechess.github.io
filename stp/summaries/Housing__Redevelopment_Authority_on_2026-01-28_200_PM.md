#### Meeting Info
* Type: Housing and Redevelopment Authority (HRA) meeting
* Purpose: To discuss and vote on various items, including the tenant protections ordinance and Conduit Revenue Bonds for Episcopal Homes project
* Date/Time: 2026-01-28, 2:00 PM
* Attendees: Chair Chaniqua Johnson, Vice Chair Jost, Commissioners Bowie, Coleman, Kim, Noecker, Yang, Director McMahon, Jenny Wolf (PED), Tom Henry (Episcopal Homes CEO)

#### Summary

**Tenant Protections Ordinance**

* The HRA discussed and reviewed a presentation by Lynn Firkenhoff and Faith Dietz on the tenant protections ordinance
* The ordinance aims to provide additional protections for tenants in Saint Paul, including rent stabilization and eviction prevention
* Three full-time positions will be created to support the implementation of the ordinance: two customer service representatives and one management assistant
* The budget for these positions is $380,000, which will be funded through the Housing Trust Fund
* The implementation timeline includes pre-implementation (Jan-Apr 2026), implementation (May 2026), and post-implementation phases

**Conduit Revenue Bonds for Episcopal Homes Project**

* Jenny Wolf presented on the Conduit Revenue Bond request by Episcopal Homes to acquire a 58-unit facility at 80 McCubbin Street in Ward 1
* The HRA will issue up to $18 million in bonds, which will be used to fund the acquisition and debt service reserve
* The project is expected to create jobs and provide additional memory care units for seniors in Saint Paul
* A public hearing will be held at the HRA meeting next week, and a resolution will be considered by both the HRA board and city council

**Other Business**

* Chair Johnson thanked staff for their presentation on the tenant protections ordinance and expressed her enthusiasm for the project
* Commissioner Bowie asked about the community impact of the Episcopal Homes project and whether it would create jobs or repurpose existing staff
* Vice Chair Jost clarified the procedural process for the Conduit Revenue Bond request, noting that a public hearing will be held at the HRA meeting next week and a resolution will be considered by both the HRA board and city council
