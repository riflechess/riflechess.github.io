#### Meeting Info

* **Meeting Type:** Housing and Redevelopment Authority (HRA) meeting
* **Purpose:** To discuss and approve various items related to HRA business, including budget amendments, property sales, and developer designations.
* **Date/Time:** December 3, 2025, 2:00 PM
* **Participants:** Chair Cheniqua Johnson (participating remotely), Vice Chair Jost, Commissioner Kim, Council Member Yang, Director McMahon, Deputy Director Green, and other HRA staff.

#### Summary

The meeting began with a review of the agenda items. Item 1 was Resolution 25-1776, which approved the adoption of a second amended and restated spending plan for various tax increment financing districts. The resolution was approved unanimously (7-0).

Item 2 was Resolution 25-1854, which authorized the extension of the tentative developer designation of JB Vang Partners Inc as a developer of 694 and 680 Minnehaha Avenue East in St. Paul. This item was also approved unanimously (7-0).

Items 3 through 6 dealt with authorizing extensions for various developers to continue working on their projects, including Rondo Community Land Trust and Face-to-Face Health and Console Counseling Service Inc.

Item 7 was Resolution 25-1860, which approved the final 2026 HRA budget and certified the final HRA property tax levy payable in 2026. Chair Johnson expressed her excitement about the budget, highlighting several key investments and priorities for the city. The resolution was approved unanimously (7-0).

Item 8 was Resolution 25-1878, which amended the 2025 HRA budget to transfer unspent funds to the city general fund. This item was also approved unanimously (7-0).

Items 9 and 10 were staff reports related to the sale of Hra-owned property at 77 Congress Street East to Iglesia de Cristo and the conveyance of Hra-owned land in Chestnut Park to the City District 9, respectively.

The meeting concluded with a note of appreciation from Chair Johnson for her colleagues' help and support. Vice Chair Jost expressed his gratitude for the opportunity to chair the meetings during Chair Johnson's absence.
