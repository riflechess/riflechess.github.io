#### Meeting Info
**Meeting Type:** City Council Meeting
**Date/Time:** March 18, 2026, at 3:30 PM
**Context:** The meeting includes discussions and votes on various resolutions and ordinances, including a resolution recognizing Eid al-Fatir, final adoption of an ordinance granting Xcel Energy's rezoning application, and amendments to the Tenant Protections Ordinance.

#### Summary

* **Resolution 26-461:** Recognizing Eid al-Fatir and St. Paul residents celebrating the holiday, the council voted unanimously (7 in favor) to adopt the resolution.
* **Ordinance 26-16:** Granting Xcel Energy's rezoning application, the council voted unanimously (7 in favor) to adopt the ordinance after a public hearing.
* **Amendment to Tenant Protections Ordinance (Item 18):** The council discussed and debated an amendment to temporarily extend the pre-eviction notice period from 30 days to 60 days. The Public Housing Agency expressed concerns about potential harm due to increased unpaid rent balances, which could impact their ability to access federal funding. After a detailed discussion, the council voted (7 in favor) to lay over the ordinance for one week to gather more information and allow the Public Housing Agency to provide answers to questions.
* **Ordinance 26-17:** Establishing minimum qualifications for the fire chief position, the council held a public hearing on this item and laid it over until March 25th (7 in favor) after receiving comments from a constituent who expressed concerns about the degree requirement potentially being exclusionary and inequitable.
* **Resolution Public Hearing 26-66:** Approving Allianz Field's sound level variance for the Great Lawn Concert Series, the council voted unanimously (7 in favor) to adopt the resolution after hearing from the Senior Director of Event Operations for Minnesota United and Allianz Field.
* **Resolution Public Hearing 26-67:** Authorizing the Police Department to accept the 2026 Intensive Comprehensive Peace Officer Education and Training grant, the council voted unanimously (7 in favor) to adopt the resolution.
* **Public Hearing on Grievances about Federal Immigration Operations:** The public hearing was closed after no one spoke during the allotted time (7 in favor).
* **Legislative Hearing Consent Agenda Items 23-31:** The council voted unanimously (7 in favor) to adopt the legislative hearing consent agenda items.

The meeting also included announcements and updates from ward representatives, including news about the CVS building demolition, upcoming events, and career exploration opportunities for young people.
