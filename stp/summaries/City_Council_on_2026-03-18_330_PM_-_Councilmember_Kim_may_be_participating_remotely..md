#### Meeting Info
**City Council Meeting**
* Date: 2026-03-18

* Type: Regular meeting with public hearing and legislative hearing consent agenda items
* Purpose: To discuss and vote on various ordinances, resolutions, and grant applications
* Notable attendees:
	+ Karina Serrano (Director of Housing Choice Programs for the St. Paul Public Housing Agency)
	+ Melissa Diaz (El Brito Mercado owner)
	+ Eric Hudak (Department of Safety and Inspections)

#### Summary

**Resolution 26-461: Recognizing Eid al-Fatir**
* Councilmember Kim introduced a resolution to recognize Eid al-Fatir, a significant celebration in the Islamic calendar
* The resolution acknowledges the contributions of the Muslim community to St. Paul's civic and cultural fabric
* Council members Kim, Johnson, and Coleman expressed support for the resolution
* The resolution was adopted unanimously

**Ordinance 26-16: Zoning Adjustment for Xcel Energy**
* The ordinance grants a zoning adjustment for Xcel Energy to rezone property at 325 Commercial Street from H1 Residential to I1 Light Industrial
* The council voted to adopt the ordinance without discussion or opposition

**Ordinance 26-18: Temporary Extension of Pre-Eviction Notice Period**
* Councilmember Johnson introduced an amendment to exempt the Public Housing Agency (PHA) and Section 8 properties from a temporary extension of the pre-eviction notice period
* Director Serrano testified that PHA's unique funding structure makes them vulnerable to federal funding reductions if they extend the notice period
* The council discussed the pros and cons of the exemption, including concerns about unequal treatment for tenants in other rental units
* A motion was made to lay over the ordinance for a week to allow for further discussion and research

**Ordinance 26-17: Minimum Qualifications for Fire Chief**
* HR Consulting Services Manager Chrissy Haugy presented an updated version of the ordinance establishing minimum qualifications for the fire chief position
* The changes include removing the option to substitute experience for education and requiring a bachelor's degree or higher in certain preferred fields
* Council members discussed the rationale behind the changes, including concerns about equity and access to leadership opportunities
* A public hearing was held on the item, with one speaker expressing concern about potential exclusionary effects of the degree requirement

**Legislative Hearing Consent Agenda Items 23-31**
* The council voted to adopt the legislative hearing consent agenda items without discussion or opposition

**News from the Wards**
* Council Member Johnson announced the upcoming meeting between St. Paul NAACP and Mayor Hur
* Council Member Coleman shared news about the Rondo renovation plan, including a coffee and conversations event on March 19th
* Council Member Bowie mentioned the launch of the Rondo exchange small business incubator on Selby Avenue
