#### Meeting Info
**Meeting Type:** City Council Meeting
**Date/Time:** 2026-03-18, 3:30 PM
**Attendees:** Mayor and all City Council Members (except one)
**Context:** Regular council meeting with various agenda items.

#### Summary

The meeting began with a resolution recognizing Eid al-Fatir and the Muslim community in Saint Paul. The resolution was adopted unanimously after brief comments from Council Member Kim, who highlighted the importance of celebrating diversity and promoting understanding between faiths.

Next, the council discussed Ordinance 26-16, which granted Xcel Energy permission to rezone property at 325 Commercial Street from H1 Residential to I1 Light Industrial. The ordinance was also adopted unanimously without discussion.

The main item of contention was Ordinance 26-18, which temporarily extended the pre-eviction notice period in Section 193.04 from 30 days to 60 days. Council Member Johnson proposed an amendment exempting the Public Housing Agency (PHA) and Section 8 properties from this ordinance due to potential harm to their funding.

The PHA Director, Karina Serrano, testified about the agency's concerns regarding the exemption, citing the impact on federal funding for capital projects and the need to report income loss accurately. Council Members Johnson, Coleman, Kim, Yang, and Bowie questioned the PHA about the reporting process and potential issues with tenants accessing mutual aid.

After a lengthy discussion, Council Member Coleman proposed laying over the ordinance for one week to allow additional research and follow-up questions from the council. The motion passed unanimously.

The meeting also included several other items, including:

* A public hearing on Ordinance 26-17, which established minimum qualifications for the fire chief position.
* A resolution authorizing the Police Department to accept a grant from the State of Minnesota's Office of Justice Programs.
* Public hearings on grievances related to federal immigration operations and legislative consent agenda items.

Finally, Council Members reported on news from their wards, including events in the Rondo community and the opening of a small business incubator.
