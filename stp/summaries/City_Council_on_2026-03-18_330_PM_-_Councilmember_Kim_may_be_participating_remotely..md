#### Meeting Info
* **Meeting Type:** Saint Paul City Council Meeting
* **Date/Time:** March 18, 2026, 3:30 PM
* **Attendees:** Saint Paul City Council members and staff, Director Karina Serrano from the St. Paul Public Housing Agency (PHA), representatives from Allianz Field, and other attendees.

#### Summary

**Item 16: Resolution 26-461**
The council unanimously approved a resolution recognizing Eid al-Fatir and honoring all St. Paul residents celebrating the holiday. Councilmember Kim expressed gratitude for the Muslim community's contributions to Saint Paul and emphasized the importance of inclusivity and respect for diverse faiths.

**Item 17: Ordinance 26-16**
The council adopted an ordinance granting Xcel Energy's application to rezone property at 325 Commercial Street from H1 Residential to I1 Light Industrial. This change will allow for a new light industrial development in the area.

**Item 18: Ordinance 26-18**
The council discussed a proposed amendment to temporarily extend the pre-eviction notice period in Section 193.04 of the Legislative Code from 30 days to 60 days. Director Serrano from the PHA expressed concerns that this change could harm the agency's ability to access federal funding due to increased unpaid rent balances. After a lengthy discussion, Councilmember Johnson moved to lay over the ordinance for one week to allow additional research and follow-up questions.

**Item 19: Ordinance 26-17**
The council held a public hearing on an administrative ordinance establishing minimum qualifications for the vacancy of Fire Chief. The proposed changes include requiring a bachelor's degree or higher, experience in a full-time paid fire department, and no substitution for education. Council members discussed the potential impact of these requirements on diverse leadership candidates.

**Item 20: Resolution Public Hearing 26-66**
The council approved a resolution authorizing Allianz Field to present amplified sound for the Great Lawn Concert Series event.

**Item 21: Resolution Public Hearing 26-67**
The council adopted a resolution approving the Police Department's application for the 2026 Intensive Comprehensive Peace Officer Education and Training grant from the State of Minnesota Department of Public Safety, Office of Justice Programs.

**Legislative Hearing Consent Agenda Items 23-31**
The council approved the legislative hearing consent agenda items without opposition.

**Ward Updates**

* Councilmember Johnson announced that his son was in attendance as an honorary council member in training.
* The St. Paul NAACP is hosting a meeting with the mayor at Hallie Q. Brown Community Center tomorrow (March 19).
* A coffee and conversation event about the Rondo renovation plan will be held on March 20 from 10:30 am to 12:30 pm.
* The Rondo Community Land Trust is launching their small business incubator this Saturday, March 20, at 1:30 pm.
* Councilmember Coleman announced that the CVS in Midway was finally coming down after years of efforts.

The meeting concluded with no further action items.
