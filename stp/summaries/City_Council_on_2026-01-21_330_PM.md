#### Meeting Info

* Purpose: Discussion of various city ordinances and resolutions

* Relevant Context: The meeting is taking place during a time of heightened federal immigration enforcement in the city, with many residents expressing concerns about their safety and rights.

#### Summary

The City Council met to discuss various ordinances and resolutions. Key decisions include:

* **Resolution 26-132:** Celebrating 140 years of the St. Paul Winter Carnival. The resolution was adopted unanimously.
* **Resolution 25-1914:** Outlining the city's 2026 legislative agenda. The council decided to lay over this item until February 11th for further review and discussion.
* **Resolution 26-121:** Calling on Governor Walz to pass an eviction moratorium through executive order. A version of this resolution was amended and adopted, calling for protection of not just residents but also small businesses at risk of eviction.
* **Ordinance 26-5:** Prohibiting law enforcement staging operations on city-owned properties and limiting access to non-public city spaces. The ordinance is a phased approach to address the crisis situation with federal immigration agencies in the city.

The meeting also included public hearings on various items, including:

* **Resolution Public Hearing 25-293:** Authorizing the Department of Parks and Recreation to amend the financing and spending plan for solar installation at Dayton's Bluff Recreation Center.
* **Resolution Public Hearing 25-294:** Authorizing the Department of Parks and Recreation to amend the financing and spending plan for solar installation at Frogtown Community Center.
* **Resolution Public Hearing 26-9:** Authorizing execution of a grant agreement and amending the city's 2026 operating budget to include a $100,000 donation from Unity Church Unitarian to support the work of the St. Paul Recovery Act Community Reparations Commission.

The public hearing on immigration operations was lengthy, with many residents expressing concerns about their safety and rights during the federal immigration enforcement actions in the city. Some speakers called for stronger protections, including a more robust separation ordinance and greater transparency from law enforcement.
