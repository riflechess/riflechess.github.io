#### Meeting Info
Meeting Type: City Council Meeting
Date/Time: December 3rd, 2025, 3:30 PM
Purpose: To discuss and vote on various resolutions, ordinances, and budget amendments for the city of Saint Paul.

#### Summary

The meeting began with a series of public hearings, starting with Ordinance 25-72, which proposes changes to the Mississippi River Corridor Critical Area. After some discussion, Council President Noecker proposed an amendment that would remove height restrictions downtown and restore language from the original staff recommendation. The amendment was approved.

Next, several resolutions were passed without discussion or opposition, including:

* Resolution 25-230: Establishing water charges for 2026
* Resolution 25-231: Setting water fees and charges for 2026
* Resolution 25-239: Setting sanitary steward and storm steward rates for 2026
* Resolution 25-253: Setting solid waste service fees for 2026
* Resolution 25-254: Authorizing public works to request SCORE grant funds from Ramsey County

The council then discussed and voted on various budget amendments, including:

* A2 Amendment: Requesting information regarding opioid settlement dollars
* A5 Amendment: Continuing support for the Returning Home St. Paul program
* A4 Amendment: Allocating Cultural Star dollars to keep library collections purchasing power consistent with past years

After some discussion, the council approved the 2026 budget as amended.

The meeting also included several public hearings on various resolutions and ordinances, including:

* Ordinance 25-72: Establishing Chapter 15 of the Administrative Code for structured quarterly reporting requirements
* Resolution Public Hearing 25-252: Amending home project budgets to reflect earned income from the 2024 Home Program
* Resolution Public Hearing 25-258: Approving a second amended restated spending plan for various tax increment financing districts

All public hearings were closed, and the resolutions were adopted without opposition.

The meeting concluded with some good news announcements, including events in the city's district councils and a youth coat drive hosted by Care for All.
