#### Meeting Info
* Type: City Council Meeting
* Purpose: Finalize the 2026 Budget and Approve Various Resolutions and Ordinances
* Date/Time: December 3, 2025, at 3:30 PM
* Context: This meeting follows a budget retreat held on June 5th and numerous public hearings throughout November. The council is expected to finalize the 2026 budget and approve various resolutions and ordinances related to water charges, solid waste service fees, and tax levies.

#### Summary

The City Council meeting on December 3rd was focused on finalizing the 2026 budget and approving various resolutions and ordinances. Several key decisions were made:

* The council approved the Neighborhood Sales Tax Revitalization Grant Program Awards, with Council Member Yang expressing concern about the late introduction of this item.
* Council Member Bowie withdrew Resolution 25-1896, which requested a comprehensive relocation, renovation, and reopening plan for Rondo Community Library, citing concerns about the lack of collaboration from the administration.
* The council approved several resolutions related to water charges, solid waste service fees, and tax levies, including:
	+ RES 25-230: Establishing water charges for 2026
	+ RES 25-231: Establishing water fees and charges for 2026
	+ RES 25-239: Setting the 2026 sanitary steward and storm steward rates
	+ RES 25-253: Setting the solid waste service fee for 2026
* The council approved Resolution 25-254, authorizing public works to request $828,344 in SCORE grant funds from Ramsey County to support the curbside recycling program.
* Council Member Bowie introduced amendments A2 and A5, which were related to opioid settlement dollars and funding for returning home St. Paul programs.
	+ Amendment A2 was approved after some discussion and clarification about its scope.
	+ Amendment A5 was not approved due to concerns about duplication of programs and lack of transparency in the proposed allocation of funds.
* The council approved Resolution 25-1814, adopting the 2026 budget as amended.
* Council President Noecker introduced an amendment to Item Number 42, which related to the Mississippi River Corridor Critical Area Ordinance. The amendment removed height restrictions downtown and restored language from the original staff recommendation.
	+ The public hearing on this item was continued until December 10th for final adoption.
* The council approved several other resolutions and ordinances, including:
	+ RES 25-264: Approving a five-year street reconstruction plan
	+ RES 25-271: Amending the financing and spending plans in the fire department
	+ RES 25-272: Amending the financing and spending plans in the fire department
* The meeting concluded with some good news announcements, including upcoming events and initiatives in the community.

Overall, this meeting marked an important step towards finalizing the 2026 budget and approving various resolutions and ordinances. While there were some contentious issues discussed during the meeting, the council was able to come to agreements on several key items.
