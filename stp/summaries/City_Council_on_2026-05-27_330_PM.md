#### Meeting Info
* Type: City Council Meeting
* Purpose: To discuss and vote on various city matters, including appointments, grant agreements, and policy updates
* Date/Time: May 27, 2026, 3:30 PM
* Relevant Context: This meeting is the continuation of previous discussions on various topics, including the Climate Action and Resilience Plan update.

#### Summary

The City Council meeting began with a discussion on the consent agenda items 2-11. Item 6, Resolution 26-874, was approved to appoint Iye Zhang as the Director of the Department of Safety and Inspections. The resolution passed unanimously.

Item 8, Resolution 26-886, was laid over for one week due to a request from Ramsey County.

The meeting then moved on to discuss Item 12, which involved amending license conditions for MFK Enterprise. After hearing from Mo Kia, the owner of MFK Enterprise, and Monica Schaefer from the Department of Safety and Inspections, the Council voted to adopt the department's recommendations, including a $1,000 matrix penalty.

The next item discussed was Resolution 26-841, which approved a legislative hearing fee waiver pilot program for the remainder of the state. The program aims to test whether waiving the $25 appeal fee will lead to more people attending appeals hearings.

The Council also voted on Item 14, Resolution 26-791, which approved the mayor's appointment of Mellie McMahon as the Director of Planning and Economic Development. Vice President Yang expressed her support for the appointment but noted that she had concerns about the process.

During public hearings, Kim O'Brien from the Grand Avenue Business Association spoke in favor of sound level variances for the Grand Old Day event on June 7. The Council approved the resolution with a vote of 7-0.

The meeting concluded with discussion and adoption of several resolutions related to grant agreements, including accepting $6,800,000 in home ARP funds from the United States Department of Housing and Urban Development and adopting the Climate Action and Resilience Plan update.

The plan update includes 25 action items to be prioritized over the next five years. The Council expressed support for the plan and its focus on addressing climate change and promoting sustainability. The resolution passed with a vote of 7-0.

Finally, the meeting concluded with good news from around the wards, including updates on the Walk-On-Tipi Center's grand opening, field improvement projects at Norrisdale Rec Center, Food Truck Friday, and the unveiling of a Dave Winfield statue.
