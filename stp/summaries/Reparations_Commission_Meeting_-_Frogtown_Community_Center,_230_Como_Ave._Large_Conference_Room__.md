* **Meeting Type:** Reparations Commission Meeting
* **Location:** Frogtown Community Center, 230 Como Ave. (Large Conference Room)

* **Purpose:** To discuss reparations for historical injustices in the City of Saint Paul

#### Summary

The meeting started with an introduction to the Reparations Commission's mission and goals. The commission is tasked with exploring ways to provide redress for past injustices, particularly those affecting communities of color.

Key points discussed:

* **Grant Funding:** The commission voted to apply for a $500,000 grant from the Minnesota State Legislature to support reparations initiatives.
* **Public Input:** The meeting was open to public input and several community members spoke about their experiences with racism and oppression in Saint Paul.
* **Recommendations:** A working group within the commission presented recommendations for short-term and long-term reparations initiatives, including:
	+ Creating a reparations fund
	+ Establishing a community land trust
	+ Implementing restorative justice programs

A contentious point arose when Commissioner Johnson expressed concerns about the potential costs of implementing these recommendations. Commissioner Rodriguez countered that the costs are necessary to address the historical injustices faced by marginalized communities.

The commission ultimately voted 6-2 in favor of moving forward with grant applications and exploring further reparations initiatives. Commissioners Johnson and Thompson dissented, citing concerns about fiscal responsibility.
