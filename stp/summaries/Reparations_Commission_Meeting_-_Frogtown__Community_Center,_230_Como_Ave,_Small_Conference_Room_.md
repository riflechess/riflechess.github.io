#### Summary
The meeting focused on discussing three proposed reparations initiatives:
* A $10 million allocation for community land trusts to support affordable housing in historically redlined neighborhoods.
* A proposal to establish a Saint Paul-based community wealth-building fund with an initial investment of $5 million.
* An amendment to the city's budget to allocate funds for cultural preservation and revitalization projects.

The commission also received input from local residents regarding their concerns about gentrification and displacement in Frogtown.

**Controversy:** A heated discussion arose when a proposal was made to add language to the reparations package that would require private developers to adhere to certain community benefits agreements. Some commissioners argued that this would be an overreach of municipal authority, while others saw it as a necessary measure to ensure equitable development practices in the city.

**Votes:**
* Resolution 1 (affordable housing allocation): 5-2 in favor
	+ Commissioner Thompson opposed due to concerns about bureaucratic red tape and potential unintended consequences.
	+ Commissioner Lee abstained, citing need for more research on community land trusts' effectiveness.
* Resolution 2 (community wealth-building fund): Unanimous approval
* Resolution 3 (cultural preservation and revitalization projects): 4-1 in favor
	+ Commissioner Patel opposed due to concerns about potential misallocation of funds.

The meeting concluded with a vote to forward the reparations package to the city council for further discussion and consideration.
