#### Meeting Info
**Type:** Housing & Redevelopment Authority (HRA) Meeting
**Purpose:** Reviewing housing developments, emergency rental assistance data, and state funding applications.
**Date/Time:** June 10, 2026, at 2:00 PM
**Location:** Saint Paul Council Chambers

#### Summary
The meeting covered several key items regarding local housing development, resident support programs, and state-level funding preparations.

**Development Projects & Land Use**
*   **University Corridor Site:** A resolution was passed (7-0) to move forward with the acquisition of a site on the university corridor. The project involves future demolition and plans for public investment in the area.
*   **1087 Ross Avenue (Item 2):** Plans were presented for a duplex at this HRA-owned property. The development, by M&M Real Estate Development LLC, will feature two three-bedroom, two-bath units aimed at families earning up to 120% of the area median income.
    *   **Budget/Funding:** Total project costs are approximately $906,800. The committee discussed a "development gap" of $391,800. Because this exceeds current program caps, it will require specific approvals on June 24th.

**Public Safety & Quality of Life**
*   **Emergency Rental Assistance Program (ERAP):** Staff provided an update on the program designed to prevent evictions by paying landlords directly for residents under certain income thresholds.
    *   **Participation:** During April and May, 257 applications were received, with 47 processed through a lottery system resulting in 31 payouts of approximately $87,226.
    *   **Fraud Prevention:** There was significant discussion regarding the emergence of "AI-generated" fraudulent applications. The HRA is currently screening these out before the lottery process to ensure that funds reach legitimate Saint Paul residents.
    *   **Staffing:** The program is currently operating with limited staff due to hiring freezes and personal leaves; full staffing capacity for this program is expected by August.

**State Funding & Partnerships**
*   **Minnesota Housing Finance Agency (MHFA) Preparations:** The board reviewed "reservations of funds" for six specific projects. These reservations are a procedural step to ensure these projects can secure necessary state tax credits and loans:
    1.  **HAMS West End Apartments:** 70 units (affordable).
    2.  **Gloryville:** 69 units plus a planned 20,000-square-foot grocery store.
    3.  **Face-to-Face:** 24 units of permanent supportive housing for youth.
    4.  **Westminster Vista.**
    5.  **The Aragon:** 53 units.
    6.  **Ramsey Hill:** 54 units across six buildings.

**Noteworthy Discussions**
*   The vote regarding the University corridor acquisition was **unanimous (7-0)**, with no opposition noted during the proceedings.
*   While there were no heated arguments, there was a detailed inquiry regarding how the city handles "robotic" or AI-driven applications for rental assistance to ensure the integrity of the program and protect residents from fraud.
