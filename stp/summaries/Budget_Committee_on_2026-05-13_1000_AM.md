#### Meeting Info
Meeting Type: Budget Committee
Purpose: Review of Ramsey County's biennial budget process and telecast services partnership with St. Paul Neighborhood Network
Date/Time: 2026-05-13, 10:00 AM
Present: Chair Johnson, Council Members Jost, Aker, Kim (absent), Bowie (expected), County Manager Becker, Deputy County Manager Kutz, Director Wilms

#### Summary

The Budget Committee meeting focused on the Ramsey County's biennial budget process and its partnership with St. Paul Neighborhood Network for telecast services. 

County Manager Becker explained the benefits of a biennial budget cycle, including reduced budget fatigue, improved strategic planning, and enhanced performance evaluation. The county's biennial budget process involves a two-year calendar with a focus on performance metrics in odd years and spending priorities in even years.

Council President Aker asked about predicting gaps in future budgets, and Deputy County Manager Kutz explained that the county uses stable revenue streams and projected bargaining agreements to estimate future revenues. He also mentioned the importance of flexibility in adjusting to unexpected changes.

County Manager Becker shared the county's reserve policy, which aims for at least two months of operating funds. However, if additional funding is needed to close a gap or address an unforeseen expense, the county board must approve it.

The committee discussed community engagement and how it factors into the budget process. County Manager Becker emphasized that departments engage with their communities throughout the year through budget narratives, framework plans, and town halls. The county also conducts public hearings on budget-related matters.

In terms of telecast services, OTC Director Chase Maxwell presented an update on the partnership with St. Paul Neighborhood Network (SPIN) for YouTube simulcasting. He announced that starting next week, City Council meetings will be available on YouTube, and SPIN staff are working closely with OTC to manage meeting production duties.

Council President Noecker asked about the financial implications of the partnership and how it would impact city staff's workload. Director Maxwell explained that approximately 300 hours of staff time per year would become available for other communication support tasks.

The committee also touched on the county's participation in the partnership, with Council President Noecker asking why they weren't involved earlier. County Manager Becker clarified that the county was still debating whether to use YouTube simulcasting and wanted to manage their own changeover process.

Finally, Director Wilms expressed appreciation for the partnership with OTC and highlighted the importance of having a dedicated communications lead to unlock the capacity of both parties in partnering more sustainably.

No contentious votes were taken during this meeting.
