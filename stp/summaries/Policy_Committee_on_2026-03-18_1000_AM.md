#### Meeting Info
* Type: Policy Committee Meeting
* Purpose: Discussion on Special Assessment Policies and Procedures for Street Maintenance and Improvement Projects, as well as Licensing and Contracting Processes for Solid Waste Haulers
* Date/Time: 2026-03-18, 10:00 AM
* Context: The meeting was held to discuss the city's special assessment policies and procedures, particularly in regards to street maintenance and improvement projects. Additionally, the committee discussed licensing and contracting processes for solid waste haulers.

#### Summary

The Policy Committee met on March 18th to discuss special assessments for street maintenance and improvement projects. Bruce Engelbrecht from the Finance Department presented a PowerPoint explaining how special assessments work, including the concept of value capture and special benefit. John Barclay from Hosh Appraisal discussed his firm's role in determining special benefits for properties along project areas.

Key points from the discussion on special assessments:

* Special assessments are charges placed on properties to help pay for public improvements like street maintenance or mill and overlay.
* The city uses a third-party consultant to determine special benefits, which is calculated as an increase in property value due to the improvement.
* The assessment rate is typically 25% of the project cost, but this can be reduced if the special benefit analysis shows a lower benefit.

The committee also discussed licensing and contracting processes for solid waste haulers. Director Rizzi from the Department of Regulatory Services explained that there are five different types of licenses related to solid waste hauling, including solid waste hauler and vehicle, solid waste transfer station, recycling collection center, and recycling processing center. The city has 38 active licenses in the solid waste hauler category.

Key points from the discussion on licensing and contracting:

* Licensing requirements for solid waste haulers include insurance, display of a schedule of charges, adequate fire protection and rodent control, permitted hours of operation, and proper cleaning and maintenance of vehicles.
* The city has class N and class R licenses, with class N being more heavily regulated due to greater impact on the community.

The committee also discussed contracts for solid waste services. Ms. Haas explained that the city issued a request for proposal (RFP) in 2023 for solid waste collection and recycling services, which resulted in a contract with FCC Environmental for garbage collection and Eureka for one-to-four-unit recycling. The committee discussed the process of selecting contractors and the need for transparency in procurement processes.

Key points from the discussion on contracts:

* The city has a seven-year contract with FCC Environmental for garbage collection worth $44 million.
* The city has a five-year contract with Eureka for one-to-four-unit recycling worth $61 million.
* The committee discussed the importance of evaluation and transparency in procurement processes, including the need for regular reviews of contracts and the use of independent firms to advise on contracting decisions.
