#### Meeting Info
* **Meeting Type:** Policy Committee
* **Purpose:** Discuss and update on industrial land study by Port Authority, mobile sauna proposal in parks, and legislative update from IGR Director
* **Date/Time:** 2026-05-20, 9:30 AM
* **Attendees:** Council Members, Port Authority representatives, Parks Department staff, IGR Director

#### Summary

The Policy Committee meeting started with a presentation by the St. Paul Port Authority on their industrial land study. The study found that industrial land in St. Paul is limited and finite, but can generate significant tax revenue and jobs. The Port Authority recommended creating awareness about this asset and preserving it to create more opportunities for tax base. Council Member Kim asked about policy recommendations, and the Port Authority suggested allowing lower-intensity uses on industrial land while keeping the zoning intact.

Next, the meeting turned to a proposal by Ward 5 Council Member Kim to introduce mobile saunas in parks. The Parks Department presented a plan to license mobile sauna operators, who would pay a revenue share of 10% of gross revenue. The council members raised questions about liability and insurance, but ultimately supported moving forward with the proposal.

Finally, the IGR Director provided an update on the legislative session, highlighting wins such as $13 million for West 7th Utilities and $2.5 million for the St. Paul Inclusive Playground. However, she also noted disappointments, including a lack of funding for lead water replacement and downtown redevelopment needs. The council members expressed gratitude for the work done by the IGR Director and staff, but also acknowledged that there is still much to be accomplished in the next session.

The meeting concluded with discussion on platform development for the next legislative session, and plans to involve the city council offices in the process.
