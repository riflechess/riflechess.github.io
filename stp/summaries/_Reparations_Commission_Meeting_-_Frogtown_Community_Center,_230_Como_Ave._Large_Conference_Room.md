#### Summary

The Reparations Commission meeting focused on discussing potential forms of reparations for the Rondo neighborhood, which was severely impacted by the construction of Interstate 94 in the 1960s. The commission reviewed a report highlighting the historical injustices faced by the community, including the displacement of residents and businesses.

Key discussion points included:

* Establishing a fund to support local businesses and community organizations
* Providing affordable housing options for Rondo residents
* Creating a cultural center or museum to preserve the history and heritage of the neighborhood

Commission members also discussed the importance of engaging with the broader Saint Paul community, including city officials, business leaders, and residents. They emphasized the need for a comprehensive approach that addresses both economic and social reparations.

The commission voted on two resolutions:

* Resolution 1: Establishing a $500,000 fund to support local businesses and community organizations (approved unanimously)
* Resolution 2: Creating a cultural center or museum in Rondo (tabled due to concerns about feasibility and funding)

The meeting concluded with a discussion on next steps, including further research and outreach to the community. The commission will reconvene in two weeks to continue their work.

A point of contention arose when Commissioner Johnson expressed concerns that the resolutions did not go far enough in addressing the root causes of historical injustices. Commissioner Rodriguez countered that the resolutions were a starting point for further discussion and action. While the commission ultimately voted unanimously on Resolution 1, there was clear disagreement on the scope and ambition of the reparations efforts.
