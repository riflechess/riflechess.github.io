#### Meeting Info
* Type: City Council Meeting
* Purpose: Discuss various city-related topics, including public safety, housing stability, and business development
* Date/Time: 2026-03-11, 3:30 PM
* Contextual Information: The meeting was held in response to the ongoing effects of Operation Metro Surge on St. Paul residents

#### Summary

The City Council meeting began with a review of consent agenda items, which were adopted without opposition. However, Resolution Public Hearing 26-59, authorizing the write-off of police staffing costs for qualifying free public cultural events and festivals and community events, was withdrawn due to concerns about billing accuracy.

Item number 17, first reading of Ordinance 26-17, was laid over until next week's public hearing due to staff being absent. Item number 18, second reading public hearing of Ordinance 26-16, granting the application of Xcel Energy to rezone property at 325 Commercial Street from H1 Residential to I-1 Light Industrial and amending Chapter 60 of the legislative code pertaining to the zoning map, was laid over until March 18th for final adoption.

Item number 19, Ordinance 26-18, amending Section 193.04 of the legislative code to temporarily extend the pre-eviction notice period in Section 193.04 from 30 days to 60 days, sparked significant discussion and debate among council members and public speakers. Some argued that extending the notice period would provide critical breathing room for renters to access emergency rental assistance and stabilize their households. Others expressed concerns about the potential unintended consequences of this policy, including increased debt loads for residents and disruptions to housing providers.

The council ultimately voted to adopt version two of the ordinance, which clarifies the language and specifies that the temporary extension of the pre-eviction notice period starts on May 14th and ends on December 31st. The public hearing is scheduled to continue next week at third reading unless amendments are proposed.

In other business, the council adopted Resolution Public Hearing 26-56, providing a sound level variance for the Mighty Mississippi 10K event on June 6, 2026, and Resolution Public Hearing 26-65, authorizing the city to amend project and accounting unit budgets and city special funds to align with the 2026 adopted budget.

The meeting concluded with an unusual segment featuring Girl Scouts from Groveland Elementary School, who came to City Hall to share their favorite things about St. Paul and cookies.
