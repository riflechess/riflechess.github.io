#### Meeting Info

* Relevant Contextual Information: The meeting took place during a time when the city was experiencing Operation Metro Surge, which had significant impacts on residents and businesses.

#### Summary

The City Council meeting covered various topics related to public safety, quality of life, and business proposals. 

Key decisions made include:

- The council withdrew Resolution 26-59, which authorized the write-off of police staffing costs for qualifying free public cultural events and festivals and community events.
- Ordinance 26-16 was laid over until March 18th for a second reading public hearing. It grants Xcel Energy permission to rezone property at 325 Commercial Street from H1 Residential to I-1 Light Industrial.
- The council approved version two of Ordinance 26-17, amending Section 193.04 of the legislative code to temporarily extend the pre-eviction notice period in Section 193.04 from 30 days to 60 days.
- The council voted on a 60-day eviction filing notice requirement, with several speakers testifying for and against it. Those speaking in favor argued that it would give renters more time to access emergency rental assistance and stabilize their households. Speakers opposed argued that extending the notice period would lead to unpaid balances growing and increase hardships for residents.
- The council granted an appeal related to a hotel project at United Village, approving a variance request for window and door openings on the north facade of the building.
- A public hearing was continued until March 18th regarding federal immigration operations.

Other notable discussions include:

* Ramsey County approved two months of property tax deferral for eligible households and small businesses due to Operation Metro Surge and beyond.
* Council Vice President Yang celebrated his 31st birthday during the meeting.
