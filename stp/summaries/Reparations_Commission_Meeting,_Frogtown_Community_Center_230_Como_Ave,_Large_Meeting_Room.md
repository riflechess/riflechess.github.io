#### Meeting Info
**Meeting Type:** Reparations Commission Meeting
**Purpose:** Discussion of various commission business, including the Ramsey County Community Appropriate Response initiative and potential resolutions to be sent to the City Council.
**Date/Time:** Not specified in the provided text, but assumed to be a regular meeting time for the commission.
**Location:** Frogtown Community Center, 230 Como Ave, Large Meeting Room (Saint Paul, MN)
**Attendees:**
	+ Commissioners Veronica, Carla Robinson, Nyla Golden, Traherne Cruz, Regina Jackson, and Coolia Pringle
	+ Dr. Raj (invited as a presenter on the Ramsey County Community Appropriate Response initiative)

#### Summary

The meeting began with an introduction to the presenters and discussion items for the day. Commissioner Veronica led the meeting.

**Discussion Items:**

1. **Ramsey County Community Appropriate Response Initiative:** Dr. Raj presented on the initiative, which aims to provide alternative responses to crisis situations, reducing reliance on law enforcement. The commission discussed potential ways to implement this in Saint Paul and create a community response team.
2. **Recommendation for City Council Action:** Commissioner Robinson explained that the commission can make recommendations to the City Council but ultimately, it's up to them to decide how to act on those recommendations. The commission will move forward with sending the Ramsey County Community Appropriate Response initiative resolution to the City Council for consideration.
3. **Draft Procedural Governance for Bylaws:** Commissioner Golden suggested adding a section to the agenda for new business ideas and proposing that these be discussed in the next meeting. This was met with agreement from other commissioners, who felt this would improve transparency and allow more voices to be heard.
4. **Amending Work Plan to Omit Data Collection Committee:** The commission decided to omit the data collection committee from their work plan, citing that existing data can be used for recommendations instead of creating a new committee.

**Committee Reports:**

1. **Slavery Disclosure Act Committee:** Commissioner Robinson reported on progress with drafting a resolution to disclose the history of slavery in Saint Paul and proposed a timeline for its adoption.
2. **Community Listening Session Subcommittee:** The subcommittee discussed plans for community listening sessions, focusing on ward 7 and ward 1 initially due to high concentrations of African American residents. They also explored potential venues, including Council Member Yang's district.
3. **Harm Report Committee:** The committee reviewed the importance of protecting their work through the harm report, highlighting recent incidents where similar efforts were met with backlash.

**Updates:**

1. **Anti-Black Hate Crime Resolution:** The commission voted on and will send a recommendation to the City Council regarding this resolution.
2. **Stipends:** Veronica reported that they are working to streamline the stipend process for commissioners.
3. **Reappointments:** The commission discussed reappointing Nick Muhammad, who resigned from the commission.

**Next Steps:**

1. Review and finalize the harm report contract for approval.
2. Move forward with implementing community listening sessions in ward 7 and potentially ward 6.
3. Prepare a presentation on racial equity impact assessment notes (formerly known as community impact notes) to be discussed at a future meeting.
