#### Meeting Info
**Type:** Metropolitan Arts Council and Police Foundation (MACPF) meeting
**Purpose:** Discussion of community safety initiatives, arts programming, and police-community relations
**Date/Time:** February 2026, at the West Minnehaha Recreation Center
**Attendees:** Members of the MACPD board, local artists, law enforcement representatives, and concerned citizens

#### Summary

The MACPF meeting focused on several key topics:

* **Arts Programming:** The council discussed funding for community art initiatives in underserved neighborhoods. A proposal to allocate $50,000 for a mural project in the Summit-University neighborhood was approved.
* **Public Safety:** Representatives from the Saint Paul Police Department presented an update on the city's crime reduction strategies. The department is exploring new technologies and partnerships to improve community policing.
* **Youth Engagement:** The meeting highlighted several programs aimed at engaging young people with local arts and police organizations, including a mentorship program for high school students.

A contentious issue arose during discussion of the proposed budget for 2026-2027. Council members expressed concerns over potential cuts to arts funding, citing the importance of community art projects in promoting public safety and well-being. The debate highlighted differing opinions on how to balance arts programming with more pressing public safety needs.

**Budget Allocations:**

* Approved allocation: $50,000 for mural project in Summit-University neighborhood
* Proposed cut: $20,000 from arts funding (pending further review)

The meeting concluded with a recognition of the importance of collaboration between law enforcement and community organizations to foster safer, more vibrant neighborhoods.
