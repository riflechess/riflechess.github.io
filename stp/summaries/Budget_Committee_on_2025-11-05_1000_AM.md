#### Meeting Info
* Type: Budget Committee Meeting
* Purpose: Review of budget proposals for the city's telecast services and debt management
* Date/Time: 2025-11-05, 10:00 AM
* Location: City Hall, St. Paul MN
* Context: The meeting was part of a series of departmental presentations to the Budget and Finance Committee

#### Summary

The meeting began with a presentation by Chase Maxwell, Communications and Digital Media Manager from OTC, and Bonnie Schumacher, Executive Director of SPNN, on the current telecast service model in St. Paul. They discussed the challenges faced by community media centers due to declining revenues from cable subscriptions and proposed options for modernizing telecast services.

The committee then heard a presentation by Neil Youngins, Debt and Investment Manager from OFS, on the city's debt management. He presented an overview of the city's outstanding debt portfolio, including general obligation bonds and revenue bonds, and discussed the implications of interest rate fluctuations on the city's debt obligations.

The final presentation was by Beth Commerce and Andrea Ledger, Deputy Directors of the HERO Department. They discussed the department's goals and accomplishments in 2025, including advancing equity through contracting, strengthening human rights and labor standards work, and improving employee retention. The committee also heard about the department's budget projections for 2026.

Key decisions made during the meeting include:

* Approving a proposal to reallocate PEG allocations to support the council chamber's technology fund
* Recommending a $1.5 million increase in the property tax debt levy to ensure the debt service fund is balanced over the long term
* Discussing potential refinancing opportunities for existing bonds due to interest rate fluctuations

The committee also discussed several questions and concerns raised by members, including:

* The impact of community engagement on labor standards complaints
* The process for contractors to become vendors for the city's rehabilitation loan program
* The department's budget projections for 2026 and potential changes to staffing levels
