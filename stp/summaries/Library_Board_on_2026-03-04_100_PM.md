#### Meeting Info
**Meeting Type:** Saint Paul Library Board Meeting
**Date/Time:** March 4, 2026, 1:00 PM
**Context:** The meeting focused on the renovation and updates of two libraries in Saint Paul: Hayden Heights and Rondo Community.

#### Summary

The meeting began with a resolution for public hearing 26-49, which authorized the execution of an MOU between the library and Friends of St. Paul Public Library to support the transfer of funds from Friends to the library and amend the library's financing and spending plans. The resolution was adopted unanimously after a public hearing.

Next, Director Hartman presented the Hayden Heights Library Update, discussing the renovation project's progress, design features, and timeline. Key updates included:

* The library will be closed temporarily starting March 24th for about 18 months.
* The new design includes increased visibility, flexibility in library spaces, accessible technology, connection to nature through a storytime garden and green space, and community programming areas.
* Alternative services during the closure include referring patrons to other east side libraries (Sunray and Arlington Hills) and Ramsey County Library partners.

The final item on the agenda was the Rondo Community Library Update. Director Hartman presented updates on the temporary location at Heli-Q Brown, including:

* A ribbon-cutting event in January 2026 with over 100 attendees.
* Increased program participation, despite a decrease in daily visits due to the smaller space.
* Upcoming events and partnerships, such as the Literacy Nights in partnership with Loop Van.

The discussion also covered community engagement efforts, renovations, and safety concerns. Councilor Bowie expressed excitement for the new Rondo Community Library, emphasizing its importance for the community's needs. The conversation touched on addressing safety issues through various initiatives, including improved communication between departments and community partners.

Councilors Bui and Morgan expressed their support for continued collaboration with the city to address public safety concerns in Ward 1. Director Hartman acknowledged ongoing efforts to improve safety measures around the library, while also promoting community engagement and partnerships.

The meeting concluded with a discussion on the bigger picture of public safety, which was deemed a great topic for exploration by the Public Safety Committee. The adjournment ended the meeting, and Councilor Jost invited attendees to attend the rebrief event the next day, featuring a panel discussion on this year's book selection, Louisiana.
