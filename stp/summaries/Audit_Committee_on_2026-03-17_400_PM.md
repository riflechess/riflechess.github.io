#### Summary
The meeting began with an introduction of new committee members, Jeremy Lostetter and Noah McVeigh. The chairperson, Shaniqua Dylan, explained that the committee's current audit is taking longer than expected due to setbacks in the Wilder Research project.

Dr. Heather Britt from Wilder Research presented an update on the data practices study, explaining the methodology and timeline for the report. She discussed the challenges faced by her team in collecting data, including difficulties in contacting other jurisdictions and obtaining responses to surveys. The committee members asked questions about the survey's methodology and sampling frame, with Dr. Britt explaining that they are targeting 700 individuals who have made requests through the city's portal system.

Noah McVeigh provided an update on community engagement efforts, reporting that he has met with eight district councils and received positive feedback from their executive directors. He discussed potential new topics for audit, including a directory of programs and services by functional area and department, and proposed creating mailing materials to distribute information about the committee's work.

The meeting concluded with discussion on selecting a new topic for audit, with members considering ideas submitted through an evergreen survey on the committee's website. The chairperson suggested reviewing the list of topics and coming back to the next meeting in May to discuss further.
