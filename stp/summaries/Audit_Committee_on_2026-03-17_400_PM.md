#### Meeting Info
* Type: Audit Committee meeting
* Purpose: To discuss the progress of ongoing audits and to select a new audit topic for 2026
* Date/Time: March 17, 2026, 4:00 PM
* Relevant Contextual Information: The mayor and her team attended the previous meeting to discuss potential audit topics

#### Summary

The meeting began with an introduction from Shaniqua Dylan, the Ward 7 City Councilmember. She welcomed new committee members Dr. Donnelly, Jeremy Lostetter, and Noah McVeigh. They discussed their backgrounds and experiences.

Dr. Britt from Wilder Research presented an update on the data practices study. The committee is conducting a performance audit of the city's data practice request process, including GOV QA system capabilities, reporting and data trends, resident-facing portal experience, policies, training, and compliance. Dr. Britt discussed the challenges faced in collecting data due to external factors.

The committee then moved on to discuss community engagement with Mr. McVeigh, who presented a memo detailing his interactions with district councils. He reported that the councils are enthusiastic about the audit committee's work and have provided suggestions for future topics, including a directory of city programs and services by functional area and department.

The discussion centered around improving communication and outreach to the community, including creating mailing materials and newsletters. Mr. McVeigh suggested having short autobiographies on the landing page to give a human face to the committee's work. He also proposed creating a blog that summarizes periodic updates on the audit committee's projects.

Councilmember Johnson noted the variance in knowledge of the audit committee's existence across different districts, with more awareness on the northwest side and less on the east side. The committee discussed possible topics for future audits, including college bound St. Paul, which was suggested by the mayor during their previous meeting.

The meeting concluded with an agreement to review the submitted topic ideas, discuss the conversation with the mayor, and come back to the next meeting in April prepared to narrow down the list of potential audit topics.

Notable decisions or proposals:

* The committee will create a directory of city programs and services by functional area and department.
* Mr. McVeigh will work on organizing this directory over the next couple of months.
* The committee will have short autobiographies on the landing page to give a human face to their work.
* They will consider creating a blog to summarize periodic updates on their projects.
* The mayor suggested college bound St. Paul as a potential audit topic, and the committee will discuss this further.

Contentious or divided votes:

* None noted in the meeting summary.
