#### Meeting Info
Title: City Council on 2025-12-17 3:30 PM
Date and Time: December 17, 2025 at 3:30 PM

#### Summary
The meeting began with the introduction of several resolutions to honor public figures. Russ Stark was recognized for his service as a council member and president, and Mayor Melvin Carter III was honored for his dedication to the city. Chief Barton Butch Inks was also celebrated for his 40 years of service to the city.

A resolution recognizing the 50th anniversary of the Hmong community's presence in the United States was introduced by Council Member Yang. The resolution acknowledged the sacrifices and contributions of the Hmong people and expressed support for their endeavors. Several speakers testified on this item, sharing personal stories and experiences of the Hmong community.

The council also discussed a public hearing regarding the establishment of the Grand and Victoria redevelopment project area and tax increment financing district. Speakers from Insight St. Paul and other organizations expressed concerns about the use of TIF funding and its impact on taxpayers.

A public hearing was held on Federal Immigration Operations and St. Paul Police Department Conduct in St. Paul, where several community members testified about their experiences with ICE and SPPD. They shared stories of violence, pepper spray, and tear gas used by police against peaceful protesters.

Council Members Yang, Kim, Bowie, Jost, Coleman, and Johnson discussed the importance of taking action on the issues raised during the public hearing. They mentioned entering a public hearing into their agenda every week to allow community members to share their grievances and experiences. The council also committed to reviewing policies and practices related to policing, including the separation ordinance.

The meeting concluded with several resolutions being adopted, including one accepting grant funds for the Office of Financial Empowerment and another authorizing the rehabilitation or raising and removal of structures at 430 Dale St. North.

Council Members expressed gratitude for their colleagues' work this year and acknowledged the challenges faced by the council. They also shared good news, including an upcoming rally in Minneapolis to support immigrant rights.
