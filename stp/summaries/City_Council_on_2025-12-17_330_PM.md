#### Meeting Info
- **Meeting Type:** City Council Meeting
- **Purpose:** Review and discuss various resolutions and ordinances, including the 2026 legislative agenda, public safety measures, and community development initiatives.
- **Date/Time:** December 17, 2025, at 3:30 PM
- **Location:** St. Paul City Hall

#### Summary

The meeting covered several important topics, including:

1.  **Celebration of Russ Stark Day**: The city council recognized and celebrated the contributions of former Council President Russ Stark to the city, particularly his work on climate action resilience plans and economic development projects.
2.  **Public Service Recognition**: The council also honored Mayor Melvin Carter III for his dedication to public service and declared December 18, 2025, as Melvin Carter Day in St. Paul.
3.  **Fire Department Recognition**: Council Vice President Kim recognized Chief Barton Butch Inks' dedication to the fire department and declared December 30th, 2025, as Chief Barton Butch Inks Day in St. Paul.
4.  **Human Rights Day and International Migrants Day**: The council passed a resolution recognizing these important dates and reaffirming support for human rights and migrants in the city.
5.  **Legislative Agenda**: Director Peterson presented the city's legislative agenda, outlining priorities such as revenue taxes and revenue, capital investment and economic development, transportation, education and workforce, public safety, housing and economic and community stability, and other organizations.
6.  **Grand and Victoria Redevelopment Project Area**: The council held a public hearing on a proposal to establish a tax increment financing district for the Grand and Victoria area, which includes a $2 million TIF contribution.
7.  **Public Hearing on Federal Immigration Operations and St. Paul Police Department Conduct**: A public hearing was held regarding the actions of the St. Paul Police Department during an ICE operation in November 2025, with several community members testifying about their experiences and concerns.

The council also discussed various resolutions and ordinances, including those related to budget adjustments, grant funding for community programs, and police overtime costs. The meeting concluded with a discussion on the city's legislative agenda and a recognition of the importance of addressing issues related to public safety, climate action, and economic development.

Some key points that were highlighted during the meeting include:

*   The need for more transparency in TIF funding and policing practices
*   Concerns about police brutality and use of force against peaceful protesters and journalists
*   Support for policies promoting affordable housing and community development initiatives
*   Importance of addressing climate change and environmental sustainability
*   Recognition of the city's commitment to protecting human rights and supporting immigrant communities
