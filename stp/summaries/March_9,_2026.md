#### Meeting Info

* **Meeting Type:** City Council Meeting
* **Purpose:** Regular monthly meeting to discuss city business, address public concerns, and make decisions on various proposals.
* **Date/Time:** Wednesday, March 9, 2026, 7:00 PM
* **Location:** Saint Paul City Hall

#### Summary

The city council meeting began with a moment of silence for the recent passing of a former city official.

**Key Decisions:**

* The council approved a $1 million allocation to upgrade the city's parks lighting system.
* A proposal to increase the tax rate by 2% to fund additional police officers and community programs was tabled until the next meeting.
* The council voted unanimously to extend the contract with the local sanitation company for an additional two years.

**Public Safety:**

* Concerns were raised about recent break-ins in the Rondo neighborhood, leading to a discussion on increasing community outreach and engagement between law enforcement and residents.
* Councilor Maria Rodriguez proposed implementing a "Safe Streets" initiative, which aims to reduce crime by improving street lighting and increasing police presence in high-crime areas. The proposal was met with mixed reactions from council members.

**Disagreement:**

A heated debate arose when discussing the tax rate increase. Councilor David Lee expressed concerns that the increased taxes would burden low-income residents, while Councilor Rodriguez argued that it's necessary to prioritize public safety. The vote on the proposal was ultimately tabled until the next meeting.

The council also heard from local business owners who expressed concerns about recent zoning changes affecting their establishments. They requested a review of the city's zoning regulations to ensure they are fair and reasonable for all stakeholders.

Overall, the meeting focused on addressing pressing issues such as public safety, infrastructure upgrades, and community engagement.
