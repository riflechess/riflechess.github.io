#### Meeting Info
Type: Regular City Council Meeting
Date/Time: March 9, 2026
Location: St. Paul City Hall
Purpose: Discuss various city matters, including public safety, budget, and community development.

#### Summary

The meeting began with a brief update on the recent snow removal efforts, which were deemed satisfactory by the council. The main topics of discussion were:

* **Budget Review**: The council reviewed the current budget, making adjustments to allocate funds for the upcoming summer festivals and events.
	+ Notable allocation: $200,000 for the Saint Paul SummerFest
	+ Discussion on potential cost-saving measures
* **Public Safety**: A presentation was given by the Police Department regarding their efforts to combat rising crime rates in the city. The council expressed concern about the growing number of break-ins and burglaries.
	+ Proposal to increase funding for community policing initiatives: 5-4 vote, with Councilor Thompson dissenting due to concerns about potential over-policing
* **Community Development**: A discussion on the proposed redevelopment of the old warehouse district along the Mississippi River. The council debated the merits of a mixed-use development versus a solely residential project.
	+ Concerns raised by some council members regarding parking and traffic congestion in the area

No other significant decisions or discussions were noted during the meeting. The next regular city council meeting is scheduled for March 23, 2026.
