#### Summary

The City Council meeting included a thorough investigation of Class A Valet Inc.'s minimum wage violation, resulting in a resolution imposing $21,658 in restitution. The investigation found that the company underpaid employees by $12 an hour and failed to provide required notice of rights to employees. After a presentation by the Human Rights Department and Assistant City Attorney Libby Kantner, the Council voted unanimously (7-0) to adopt the resolution.

The meeting also included a discussion on Ordinance 25-4, which amends Title V of the Administrative Code to add Chapter 92, titled Tree Preservation for City-Sponsored Projects. After several comments from Council Members and ward representatives, the ordinance was laid over until June 24th to allow for further public input and departmental review.

Additionally, the meeting included a series of resolutions related to public works projects, including the Wheelock Grotto Phase 2 Street Reconstruction Project, local road improvement funding from the state of Minnesota, and pedestrian improvement projects. These resolutions were approved with no opposition (7-0).

The meeting concluded with news from the wards, which included updates on upcoming events such as the Soapbox Derby, Food Truck Fridays, and a community event hosted by the St. Paul Parks Conservancy.

There was no significant disagreement or contention during this meeting, and all resolutions and ordinances were approved unanimously.
