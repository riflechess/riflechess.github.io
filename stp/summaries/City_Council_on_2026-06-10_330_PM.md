#### Meeting Info
**Type:** City Council Meeting
**Purpose:** Routine business, public hearings, and consideration of resolutions/ordinances.
**Date/Time:** June 10, 2026, at 3:30 PM.

#### Summary

**Business License Action (Maryland Supermarket)**
The Council moved to revoke the tobacco shop license for Maryland Supermarket, Inc. (doing business as Maryland Supermarket) located on Maryland Avenue. While the standard penalty was a 10-day suspension, Councilmember Kim pushed for a full revocation because the business intentionally ignored city laws by selling over 50 flavored tobacco products without the proper licensing, despite receiving a courtesy warning earlier in the year.

**Memorial Proclamation (Hortman Family)**
The City officially proclaimed June 14, 2026, as Mark, Melissa, and Gilbert Hortman Day. This was a solemn proceeding to honor former State Speaker Melissa Hortman and her family following a tragic, politically motivated attack in early 2025. Several state and local leaders attended the meeting to offer tributes to her legislative legacy and service.

**Infrastructure & Spending**
*   **Alley Improvements:** A resolution was passed to spend **$350,000** (funded by general funds and assessments) to repave a gravel alley between Hatch, Orchard, Kilburn, and Ride with bituminous pavement to address drainage and snow plowing issues.
*   **Grant Funding:** The city approved two significant state grants:
    *   A **$13,000** grant for hazardous materials emergency preparedness in the fire department.
    *   A **2027-2029 Auto Theft Prevention Program** grant from the State of Minnesota.
    *   The **Pathways to Policing Grant** was also approved.
*   **Zoo & Conservatory Support:** The council accepted a **$500,000 donation** from the nonprofit "Como Friends" to support and enhance the Como Zoo and Marjorie McNeely Conservatory.

**Community Recognition**
The Council passed several resolutions recognizing Immigrant Heritage Month, World Refugee Day, and Caribbean American Heritage Month. They also noted that they are a city of immigrants and welcomed these declarations as an affirmation of community values.

**Zoning & Development (Galtier Plaza)**
This item saw significant public pushback and discussion regarding the use of Tax Increment Financing (TIF). 
*   **The Conflict:** One resident expressed strong opposition, arguing that TIF provides "taxpayer-backed public financing with very little guaranteed in return" and compared it to previous projects that failed to provide enough lower-income units. They urged the council not to offer a "blank check" to developers.
*   **Support for Project:** The developer (Bigos Development) and local residents of the Aerie Homeowners Association expressed strong support, stating the project is necessary to revitalize the currently vacant area and would bring 166 market-rate units back to the downtown core.
*   **Resolution:** Despite the public disagreement over TIF funding methods, the Council ultimately moved forward with the resolution to establish the Galtier Plaza TIF district to facilitate the renovation of the space into housing.

**Public Safety & Nuisance Abatement**
*   **Sound Variance:** A permit was granted for the St. Paul Pride Festival (June 13-14) to allow for amplified sound at the Dual Citizen location.
*   **Property Maintenance:** The Council heard a plea from the owner of 2016 Fremont Avenue regarding "nuisance" orders. While the owner argued that they were attempting to transition the yard into a "native planting" area, the city's hearing officer recommended that the nuisance has not yet been fully abated under current state codes for managed landscapes.
