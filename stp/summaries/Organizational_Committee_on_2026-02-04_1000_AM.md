#### Meeting Info
* **Meeting Type**: Organizational Committee meeting
* **Purpose**: Review and discuss the City of Saint Paul's legislative agenda for 2026
* **Date/Time**: February 4, 2026, 10:00 AM
* **Attendees**: Director O'Rourke, Council Members Yang, Coleman, Joseph, Jost, and others

#### Summary
The meeting focused on reviewing the City of Saint Paul's legislative agenda for 2026. The agenda includes various priorities such as the arena complex, natural disaster statutes, state reimbursement program for law enforcement, West 7th Corridor transit investments, and downtown redevelopment financing tools.

Council Members discussed the importance of emphasizing economic development in areas outside of downtown, particularly in Midway. They also suggested adding a land value tax pilot project to the agenda.

The meeting also touched on administrative citations, which were approved by voters in November 2025. Director Wilms presented an overview of the process and framework for implementing administrative citations, including the establishment of a Legislative Advisory Committee. The committee will guide legislative changes and provide a written equitable implementation framework.

Council Members raised several questions and concerns about the implementation process, including the potential for punitive fines and the need for clear guidelines on dispute resolution. They also discussed the importance of balancing due process with compliance and deterrence.

The meeting concluded with a discussion on next steps, including setting up an internal working group to work through policy design considerations and creating a work plan to implement administrative citations. Council Members suggested looking at models from other cities, such as Minneapolis, to inform their decision-making.

Key decisions:

* Approved the City of Saint Paul's legislative agenda for 2026
* Established a Legislative Advisory Committee to guide legislative changes related to administrative citations
* Authorized an internal working group to work through policy design considerations and create a work plan to implement administrative citations

Notable disagreements or areas of contention:

* The potential for punitive fines and the need for clear guidelines on dispute resolution
* The balance between due process and compliance and deterrence
* The role of the Legislative Advisory Committee in implementing administrative citations
