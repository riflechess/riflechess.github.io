#### Summary

The city council meeting started with a series of resolutions and public hearings. 

First, the council adopted resolution 26-311, supporting the designation of the West 7th Corridor as the future Metro J-Line.

Next, they voted to adopt Resolution 26-330, Restoring Ford's Area C, which aims to clean up contaminated soil at a former industrial site in Highland Park.

The council then recognized Portia Hampton Flowers for her service and dedication to the city. They adopted resolution 26-343, thanking her for her career with the St. Paul City Attorney's Office.

After that, they approved Mayor Herr's appointments of policy aides, constituent services associates, and director of public engagement and constituent services through resolution 26-326.

The council also held a public hearing to commemorate the 100th anniversary of Black History Month. Council members Johnson and Bowie introduced resolution 26-58, recognizing the contributions of historically black colleges and universities (HBCUs), the National Pan-Hellenic Council, the Divine Nine, and Lynx Incorporated.

Several speakers from HBCU organizations spoke during the public hearing, including representatives from Minnesota HBCU Alumni, Alpha Kappa Alpha Sorority Incorporated, Delta Sigma Theta Sorority Incorporated, Omega Psi Phi Fraternity Incorporated, and others. They thanked the council for recognizing their contributions to the community.

After that, the council voted on a resolution (26-59) to write off police staffing costs for qualifying free public cultural festivals and community events. However, there was disagreement among council members about the resolution's legality and fairness. Some council members felt it was unfair to waive the balances of some organizations while leaving others unpaid.

Council Member Johnson moved to lay over the resolution until a two-week period, but Council Members Jost and Noecker opposed the motion. Vice President Kim supported the layover, citing concerns about the process for determining what constitutes an uncollectible debt.

The council then voted on item 17, which was a revised version of a previous proposal to establish the festivals and events security credit program. Deputy Chief Hallstrom explained how the program would work, including regular reporting to the council and quarterly updates.

Council Member Bowie moved version two of the resolution, which established the festivals and events security credit program. The motion passed with seven in favor and none opposed.

Other items discussed included ordinance 24-26 amending chapter 65 and 66 of the legislative code pertaining to drive-through sales and services, as well as several other resolutions related to budgeting and funding for various city departments.

The council also held a public hearing on item 19, which was ordinance 26-9 adopting the geothermal energy franchise by The Heights Community Energy Inc. However, there were no speakers during the public hearing.

Finally, the council approved resolution 26-38 authorizing the department of parks and recreation to amend the financing and spending plan in the amount of ten thousand dollars to enter into a grant agreement with the Minnesota Historical Society.

Throughout the meeting, several council members commended Chair Johnson for her leadership on emergency rental assistance. The council also discussed other items related to budgeting and funding for various city departments.

In addition to the main business of the meeting, there were several announcements made by Council President Noecker, including a reminder about the upcoming Skyway 5K event in downtown Saint Paul and an invitation to attend a coffee and conversation hosted by the Greater East Side Community Council.
