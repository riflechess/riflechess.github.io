#### Meeting Info

* Type: City Council Meeting
* Date/Time: February 25, 2026, 3:30 PM
* Purpose: Regular meeting of the Saint Paul City Council to discuss various items, including resolutions and ordinances.

#### Summary

The meeting began with a roll call and a moment of silence. The city council then moved on to consider several items, including:

1. **Resolution 26-311**: Designating the West 7th Corridor as the future Metro J-Line. This resolution was approved unanimously.
2. **Resolution 26-330**: Restoring Ford's Area C. This resolution was also approved unanimously.
3. **Resolution Public Hearing 26-58**: Commemorating the 100th anniversary of Black History Month and uplifting the contributions of historically black colleges and universities (HBCUs) to the city of Saint Paul. The public hearing on this item was closed, and a motion was made to adopt the resolution.
4. **Resolution Public Hearing 26-59**: Authorizing the write-off of police staffing costs for qualifying free public cultural festivals and community events. Several organizations testified on this item, including Power 104.7, Rondo Days, and others. A motion was made to close the public hearing and approve the resolution.
5. **Resolution Public Hearing 26-37**: Establishing the festivals and events security credit. This resolution was approved unanimously after a lengthy discussion about its implementation and potential issues with community trust.
6. **Ordinance 24-26**: Amending chapter 65 and 66 of the legislative code pertaining to drive-through sales and services and walk-up service windows. A motion was made to close the public hearing and approve version three of the ordinance.
7. **Resolution Public Hearing 26-42**: Modifying the financing and spending plans in the department of public works capital and capital maintenance contingency budgets, including a transfer of $90,000 for the Dale Street roof replacement. This resolution was approved unanimously.
8. **Resolution Public Hearing 26-38**: Authorizing the department of parks and recreation to amend the financing and spending plan in the amount of $10,000 to enter into a grant agreement with the Minnesota Historical Society. This resolution was also approved unanimously.
9. **Resolution Public Hearing 26-55**: Approving an increase in the parks and recreation 2026 grant fund budget by $510,000 to reflect grant funds awarded from the State of Minnesota DNR. This resolution was approved unanimously.
10. **Public Hearing on Grievances about Federal Immigration Operations**: A public hearing was held on this item, with several speakers testifying about their concerns regarding federal immigration operations in Saint Paul.

Additionally, the city council:

* Approved a consent agenda that included several resolutions and ordinances.
* Heard from various city officials, including the mayor and department directors, about ongoing projects and initiatives.
* Discussed and approved a resolution to invest additional resources into the emergency rental assistance program.
* Recognized the contributions of historically black colleges and universities (HBCUs) to the city of Saint Paul.
