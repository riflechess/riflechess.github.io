#### Meeting Info
**Meeting Type:** Policy Committee Meeting
**Date/Time:** 2026-02-18, 10:00 AM
**Purpose:** Discussion and review of the bike rack permit program and climate action resilience plan.

#### Summary

The meeting began with a presentation by Jimmy Schoemaker from Public Works on the bike rack permit program. The program aims to formalize the process for installing custom-branded bike racks in the public right-of-way, allowing businesses and district councils to purchase and install them. The program would require a permit and would ensure that installations are done by qualified private contractors. Council members asked questions about design considerations, maintenance responsibilities, and cost estimates.

Next, Liz Boyer presented the climate action resilience plan (CARP 2.0). The plan aims to reduce greenhouse gas emissions in the city of St. Paul by 2050. Key features include:

* A 1% allocation of the city's budget for climate action initiatives
* A five-year action plan with 25 priority actions, including expanding EV car share and bike parking facilities
* An emphasis on equity and inclusion, particularly in addressing environmental justice issues

Council members raised questions about the 1% funding target, how it would be achieved, and what specific actions would be taken to address racial and socioeconomic disparities. They also discussed potential partnerships with Ramsey County and the importance of clear communication about climate-related initiatives.

Some key takeaways from the meeting include:

* The bike rack permit program aims to formalize a process for installing custom-branded bike racks in public right-of-way.
* CARP 2.0 proposes allocating 1% of the city's budget for climate action initiatives and prioritizing equity and inclusion.
* Council members expressed concerns about how to prioritize neighborhoods most vulnerable to environmental hazards and emphasized the importance of clear communication about climate-related initiatives.

Overall, the meeting provided a detailed review of two key policy initiatives: the bike rack permit program and CARP 2.0. The council will continue to discuss these initiatives as they move forward with implementation and budget planning.
