#### Meeting Info
**Type:** Regular City Council Meeting
**Date/Time:** November 5, 2025, 3:30 PM
**Purpose:** To discuss various items including liquor license applications, reparations commission updates, historic preservation designations, and public safety measures.

#### Summary

* The meeting began with a Pledge of Allegiance and a review of the consent agenda.
* Item 11, resolution 25-1677, was approved to grant an Entertainment B license and expand liquor service areas for the University of St. Thomas.
* Council Member Coleman spoke in favor of the license, citing its benefits for the university and the community.
* The council also discussed and approved a revised version of Resolution 25-1728, establishing the membership of the Reparations Harm Study RFP Review Committee.
* Council Members Johnson and Bowie engaged in a discussion about the reparations commission's work, with Bowie expressing concerns about the process and Johnson moving to approve a revised resolution.
* The council also honored City Clerk Sherry Moore for her nearly four decades of dedicated public service, passing a resolution proclaiming November 5th as "Sherry Moore Day."
* A series of ordinances related to cannabis and hemp businesses were laid over to November 12th for second reading public hearing.
* A policy session on virtual currency kiosks was also discussed, with the council introducing an ordinance to ban their placement and operation in St. Paul.
* Public hearings were held on several items, including a resolution ratifying assessments for various areas, water fees and charges for 2026, and sanitary sewer and storm sewer rates.
* The council heard testimony from residents and stakeholders regarding the demolition of a CVS building at 499 Snelling Avenue North, with many calling for its removal due to nuisance concerns.
* A motion was passed to approve the legislative hearing officer's recommendation to order the raising and removal of the structure within 15 days.
