#### Meeting Info
**Transportation Committee Meeting**
* Date/Time: Wednesday, March 22nd, 6:00 PM
* Location: Saint Paul City Hall, Conference Room A
* Purpose: Discussion and decision-making on transportation-related issues within the city of Saint Paul

#### Summary
The Transportation Committee meeting covered several topics, including updates on ongoing projects and new proposals.

1. **Update on the 3rd Street Reconstruction Project**: The committee heard from project managers about the progress made so far. While the project is ahead of schedule, there were some concerns raised by committee members regarding the impact of construction on local businesses.
2. **New Bike Lane Proposal for Rice Street**: A proposal to install a bike lane on Rice Street was presented to the committee. Members discussed potential issues with parking and traffic flow, but generally expressed support for the idea.
3. **Increased Funding Request for Public Transit**: The city's public transit system requested an increase in funding to improve service frequency and expand routes. Committee members debated the merits of allocating more funds, considering both financial constraints and community needs.

A contentious point arose during the discussion on public transit funding, with some committee members arguing that increased spending would be too costly for taxpayers. Others countered that improved public transportation is essential for reducing congestion and promoting economic growth in underserved neighborhoods.

The committee ultimately voted to recommend increased funding for public transit, but with a condition for further review of operational costs and potential cost savings measures.
