#### Meeting Info
**Meeting Type:** Housing and Redevelopment Authority (HRA) meeting
**Purpose:** To discuss and approve budget proposals, tentative developer designations, and other related matters
**Date/Time:** November 19, 2025, 2:00 PM
**Context:** The meeting is part of the HRA's regular public meetings, where they discuss and vote on various issues related to housing and redevelopment in Saint Paul.

#### Summary

The meeting covered several topics, including:

1. **Tentative Developer Designations:** The HRA discussed extensions for four projects: Hams Project, Gloryville Face to Face, 1036 Marshall (Rondo Community Land Trust), and Gloryville. These extensions will allow the developers more time to complete due diligence and finalize financing.
2. **Promise Act Grant Program:** Shahir Ahmed from Neighborhood Development Center presented an update on the Promise Act grant program, which aims to support entrepreneurs in underserved communities. The first round of grants awarded $9.5 million to 650 eligible applicants, with 83% being BIPOC entrepreneurs.
3. **2026 HRA Budget Proposal Introduction:** Director McMahon introduced the proposed 2026 budget for the Housing and Redevelopment Authority (HRA). The proposal includes changes in funding sources, allocations, and recommendations for future investments.
4. **Downtown Vitality Fund:** Commissioner Noecker presented a proposal for a $5 million downtown vitality fund, which aims to catalyze residential and retail development in downtown Saint Paul. The proposal includes two pools of funds: one for new housing development ($3 million) and another for street-level retail ($2 million).

Key decisions made during the meeting include:

* Approval of extensions for four tentative developer designations
* Introduction of the 2026 HRA budget proposal
* Discussion and consideration of the downtown vitality fund proposal

Notable disagreements or arguments during the meeting include:

* Commissioner Kim mentioned that some of the funding proposals may not be feasible due to restrictions on LAHA dollars.
* Commissioner Yang asked for clarification on whether the downtown alliance has secured support from PED, and Commissioner Noecker stated that they have had conversations with the Downtown Alliance but still need to confirm support from PED.
