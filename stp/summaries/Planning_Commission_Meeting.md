#### Meeting Info
**Type:** Planning Commission Meeting
**Purpose:** Review and discuss zoning requests, land use changes, and development proposals
**Date/Time:** Wednesday, March 15, 2023 at 6:00 PM
**Location:** City Hall, Room 300
**Attendees:** Commissioner Johnson (Chair), Commissioners Rodriguez, Lee, and Wang, plus representatives from the Planning Department

#### Summary
The meeting began with a public hearing on a zoning request for a new apartment complex in the Highland Park neighborhood. The proposal was met with some opposition from nearby residents concerned about increased traffic and density.

Key Decisions:
* The commission voted 4-1 to approve the zoning request, with Commissioner Lee dissenting.
* A motion to approve a land use change for a downtown Saint Paul property was tabled due to concerns about parking and pedestrian safety.
* The commission discussed and approved a new development proposal for the East Side of St. Paul, which includes a mix of residential and commercial units.

Points of Contention:
* Commissioner Lee expressed concerns about the Highland Park zoning request, citing potential impacts on local infrastructure and community character.
* A nearby resident spoke out against the apartment complex, arguing that it would lead to increased noise and disruption.
* The commission discussed possible solutions for addressing parking and pedestrian safety concerns related to the downtown land use change.

Next Steps:
* The approved zoning request will be forwarded to City Council for further review.
* The development proposal for the East Side of St. Paul will move forward with final design plans to be submitted in April.
