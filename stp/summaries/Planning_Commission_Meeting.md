#### Meeting Info
**Meeting Type:** Planning Commission Meeting
**Purpose:** Discuss zoning changes, urban renewal projects, and development proposals in various neighborhoods of Saint Paul
**Date/Time:** Tuesday, March 21st, 2023, 6:00 PM - 9:00 PM
**Location:** City Council Chambers, 350 4th St E, Saint Paul, MN 55101

#### Summary
The Planning Commission meeting covered several key topics:

* **Zoning Changes:** The commission discussed and approved zoning changes for the Frogtown neighborhood to accommodate new development projects. These changes include updates to the Mixed-Use Zoning Code.
	+ Some community members expressed concerns about increased density, but the commission voted 8-2 in favor of the proposed changes.
* **Urban Renewal Projects:** The commission reviewed proposals for urban renewal projects in the Summit-University and Rondo neighborhoods. Funding allocations were discussed:
	+ $1 million allocated for street improvements in the Summit-University area
	+ $500,000 set aside for a community garden project in the Rondo neighborhood
* **Development Proposals:** Two development proposals were presented: one for a new residential building near the University of Saint Thomas and another for a commercial development on Selby Avenue.
	+ Community members raised concerns about gentrification and displacement of long-time residents, but the commission voted to approve both projects with conditions to ensure affordable housing units.

Note: Commissioner Jenkins expressed concerns about the potential impact of increased development on the city's affordable housing stock. Commissioner Patel countered that the proposed projects will bring much-needed investment to underserved neighborhoods. The discussion highlights ongoing tensions between economic growth and community needs.
