#### Meeting Info
* **Meeting Type:** Budget Committee
* **Purpose:** Review of TIF (Tax Increment Financing) districts, including decertification process and allocation of funds for affordable housing.
* **Date/Time:** 2026-06-03, 10:00 AM
* **Location:** Live and Recorded Public meetings for the City of Saint Paul

#### Summary

The meeting focused on reviewing TIF districts and their implications on the city's budget. The main topics discussed were:

1. Decertification Process:
	* TIF districts have a statutory length for collections, which is 26 years.
	* Pooling limits the spending within the TIF district, restricting the use of collected tax increments after 5 years from certification.
	* If the qualifying expenditures not deemed limited by pooling are completed and fall below 65% of actual collections, the district must be decertified.
2. Tax Capacity:
	* The city's tax rate would decline if a TIF district is decertified, as the captured tax capacity is returned to the tax base.
	* The release of tax capacity could result in an increase to the levy, maintaining the same tax rate.
3. HRA (Housing and Redevelopment Authority) Board:
	* The HRA only keeps a district open to pool for affordable housing if it meets its development objectives and covers all obligations.
	* A district may be kept open to pool tax increments for qualifying housing projects, but income restrictions apply.
4. TIF Districts in Saint Paul:
	* There are currently 47 TIF districts in the city, with five that have obligations fully paid by the end of last year.
	* Three districts (Emerald Park, Phelan Village, and Pioneer Endicott) will be proposed for early decertification due to maximized pooling.
5. CDBG (Community Development Block Grant):
	* The city's allocation for CDBG has been decreasing over the years, with a current allocation of $6.7 million.
	* Funds are allocated through the Consolidated Plan, which guides spending on five-year plans.
	* The Consolidated Plan process involves identifying goals and allocating funds to match those goals.

Some notable discussions and disagreements during the meeting include:

* Council Vice President Yang expressed concern about the lack of transparency regarding TIF districts that can be decertified early. She suggested considering a policy change to notify council members when such districts are up for consideration.
* Director McMahon was asked to join the discussion to clarify the process for determining blight and creating TIF districts in areas where developers are not interested.
* Council President Naker questioned the use of 10% as a cap on the amount of tax capacity that can be captured by TIF districts, asking if it's a legal requirement or a city policy.

The meeting concluded with a review of the CDBG program and its allocation process.
