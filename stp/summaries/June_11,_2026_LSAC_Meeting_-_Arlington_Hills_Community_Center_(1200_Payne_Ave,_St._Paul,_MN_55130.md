#### Meeting Info
**Type:** Local Safety Advisory Committee (LSAC) Meeting
**Purpose:** Discuss public safety concerns and community issues in the Arlington Hills neighborhood
**Date/Time:** June 11, 2026, 7:00 PM - 9:00 PM
**Location:** Arlington Hills Community Center, 1200 Payne Ave, St. Paul, MN 55130

#### Summary
The LSAC meeting focused on various public safety and community concerns in the Arlington Hills neighborhood.

* The committee discussed a proposed partnership with the local library to create a "safe zones" program, which would provide a secure space for residents to report crimes or suspicious activity. After some discussion, the proposal was tabled until the next meeting.
* There was a lengthy debate about the recent increase in property damage and vandalism in the area. Councilor Johnson expressed frustration with the lack of action from city officials to address these issues, while Councilor Patel argued that more resources were needed to combat this problem effectively.
* The committee voted 5-2 to allocate an additional $10,000 to enhance community outreach and engagement initiatives, with some members expressing concerns about the cost-effectiveness of such programs.

A notable moment during the meeting was a presentation by local resident Maria Rodriguez, who shared her personal experience of being a victim of property damage. Her emotional testimony sparked a passionate discussion among committee members about the need for more resources to prevent such incidents in the future.
