#### Summary

The meeting began with the adoption of the consent agenda items 3 through 9. Item 5, Resolution 25-1721, was discussed in detail regarding the transfer of $40,000 from an unspent project to the Library Optimal Response Project for contracted security at Rondo Library. The resolution passed unanimously.

Next, Item 8, Resolution 25-1746, was pulled off the consent agenda for discussion on the city general election held November 4, 2025. Council President Kim clarified that the city ordinance does not require manual counting of ballots and addressed public comments and questions raised by citizens.

The meeting then moved to Item number 9, which was suspended due to a clerical error in Legistar. Resolution 25-1780, appointing special counsel for the city's lawsuit against the Trump administration, was approved unanimously.

Item number 10, Ordinance 25-59, amending traditional neighborhood district zoning study chapters, was passed with seven votes in favor and none opposed.

Council Member Jost introduced Item number 9 as a T-district follow-up and transit corridor zoning study. The resolution aimed to simplify the development process and provide tools for district councils. It passed unanimously.

Council Member Kim shared her personal experience with gun violence and proposed an ordinance regulating firearms, which was also approved unanimously. Vice President Kim emphasized the need for state legislation to address gun control and encouraged citizens to participate in the election cycle.

The meeting then shifted to a public hearing section, where several ordinances were laid over for further discussion:

* Ordinance 25-77: Amending Section 220.05 of the Legislative Code to set rates for base-level garbage service
* Ordinance 25-63: Allowing garbage cart sharing at properties with two or more dwelling units
* Resolution public hearing 25-253: Setting the solid waste service fee for 2026

The meeting concluded with the introduction of various legislative consent agenda items, including RLHSAO 25-62 and RLHSAO 25-60.
