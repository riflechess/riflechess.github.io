#### Meeting Info
**Meeting Type:** City Council Meeting
**Date/Time:** 2025-11-12, 3:30 PM
**Purpose:** Discussion and vote on various city council items, including resolutions, ordinances, and public hearings.

#### Summary
The meeting began with a discussion of consent agenda items, which were passed with some modifications. The main topics of discussion included:

* **Resolution 25-1721**: Amending project budgets to align with spending needs, specifically transferring $40,000 from the DSI SAC fees project to the Library Optimal Response Project.
	+ Council Member Bowie asked questions about the transfer and clarified that it was due to a change in the timing of the library's move. Deputy Director Laura Logsdon explained that the funds were initially allocated for this year but are now needed next year.
* **Resolution 25-1746**: Canvassing the returns and declaring the results of the city general election held November 4, 2025.
	+ Council Member Jost raised concerns about the voting process and asked for discussion on manual counting of ballots. Ramsey County confirmed that it is up to the city to decide whether their ranked voting system is acceptable.
* **Resolution 25-1780**: Appointing Jacobson Lawyers Group as special counsel representing the city in a lawsuit against the Trump administration.
	+ This was passed under suspension due to a clerical error.

The council also discussed and voted on several ordinances, including:

* **Ordinance 25-59**: Amending Chapters 60, 63, 65, 66, 67, and 69 of the Legislative Code as recommended in the traditional neighborhood district zoning study.
	+ This was passed with a motion for approval from Council Member Johnson.
* **Resolution 25-113**: Initiating the T-district follow-up and transit corridor zoning study.
	+ This was also passed with a motion for approval from Council Member Jost.

The meeting also included public hearings on various items, including:

* **Ordinance 25-63**: Amending legislative code chapter 220 to allow for garbage cart sharing at properties with two or more dwelling units beginning January 1st, 2026.
	+ There were no speakers during the public hearing, and a motion was made to close the public hearing and lay the matter over until November 19th.

In addition, the council discussed and voted on several other items, including:

* **Resolution 25-253**: Setting the solid waste service fee for 2026.
	+ This was passed with a motion from Council Member Jost to close the public hearing and approve.
* **RLHSAO 25-60**: Appeal of Emmett J. Owens Jr. to a summary abatement order and vehicle abatement order at 239 Bates Avenue.
	+ This was referred to legislative hearings on December 9th.

The meeting also included discussion on public safety, specifically related to gun violence prevention. Council Member Jost shared her personal experience with gun violence and the need for action. The council passed an ordinance to regulate assault weapons, binary triggers, ghost guns, and signage in city parks, libraries, schools, and homes.

Lastly, the meeting included a discussion on virtual currency kiosks, with AARP presenting concerns about these machines being used as vehicles for delivering money to scammers. Bitcoin Depot also presented, sharing their industry-leading anti-fraud policies and urging the council to work with them to enact common-sense reforms that further protect consumers and foster innovation in Minnesota.
