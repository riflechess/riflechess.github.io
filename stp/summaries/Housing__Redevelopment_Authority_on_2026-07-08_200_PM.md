#### Meeting Info
**Type:** Housing & Redevelopment Authority (HRA) Public Meeting  
**Purpose:** Discussion of loan modifications for affordable housing projects and proposed land use leases for transit infrastructure.  
**Date/Time:** July 8, 2026, at 2:00 PM  
**Context:** City of Saint Paul HRA meeting regarding property management and development funding.

#### Summary
The meeting covered two primary items involving property management and logistics:

**TWV Limited Partnership Loan Amendment**
*   **Project Scope:** The TWV partnership currently includes three properties: Torre de San Miguel, Westminster Place, and Vista Village. 
*   **Modification Request:** Because the projects have different rehabilitation needs and funding paths, the HRA is reviewing a request to remove "Torre de San Miguel" as collateral from the original CDBG loan.
*   **Reasoning:** Moving Torrey into its own funding structure reduces costs and simplifies the compliance process for the project's upcoming financial close in November 2026.
*   **Risk Assessment:** Staff confirmed that even with the removal of Torrey as collateral, the HRA remains in the first lien position on the remaining properties. The current loan-to-value ratio is low (approximately 5.65%), meaning the risk to the city remains minimal. This change will not impact the operations of Westminster Place or Vista Village.

**Union Pacific Railroad (UPRR) Lease Agreement**
*   **Property:** A gravel parking lot at 43 Water Street consisting of five parcels.
*   **Proposed Lease:** A three-year lease to Union Pacific Railroad for use during the reconstruction of the Mississippi River Lift Bridge.
*   **Community Impact/Quality of Life:** The primary goal of this lease is to keep construction workers and equipment trailers off the streets, thereby reducing the impact on the surrounding neighborhood. 
*   **Financials & Future Use:** 
    *   The HRA currently receives about $11,000 annually from current parking operations.
    *   The proposed UPRR lease would provide approximately $19,200 annually ($1,600 per month).
    *   Due to the lack of a proper drainage system, the land is not currently suitable for public parking without significant investment. After the three-year lease ends, there is an intent to work with the Parks Department to transition the space into community event grounds (such as for the Yacht Club Festival).
