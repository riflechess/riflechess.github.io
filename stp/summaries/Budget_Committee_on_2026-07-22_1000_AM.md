#### Meeting Info
- **Type:** Budget Committee Meeting
- **Purpose:** Discussion of city budget priorities regarding street lighting infrastructure and road maintenance programs.
- **Date/Time:** July 22, 2026, at 10:00 AM
- **Context:** City of Saint Paul

#### Summary
**Streetlight Wire Theft & Public Safety**
A significant portion of the meeting was dedicated to the ongoing issue of copper wire theft from streetlights. This is a high-priority public safety and quality-of-life issue, as it leaves streets dark and puts residents at risk.

*   **Current Status:** Approximately 3,500 lights are currently out due to theft. Because many lights share a single circuit (ranging from 20 to 80 lights), one act of theft can darken a large area.
*   **Budget & Funding:** There is no dedicated funding for wire theft repairs; these costs are currently being pulled from the general street lighting budget, which results in "deferred maintenance" on other necessary infrastructure improvements.
*   **Attempted Deterrents:** The city has experimented with several methods to stop thieves, including reduced wire sizes and physical guards (which were eventually bypassed). Solar lights were trialed but are not considered a viable city-wide solution due to high upfront costs and the ongoing need for battery maintenance.
*   **Strategic Outlook:** The city is moving toward an "enterprise approach" that involves coordination between Public Works, Police (increasing prosecution penalties), and community awareness.

**Contention & Discussion Points**
There was specific discussion regarding a proposed **"street lighting improvement district."** While this would create a structured way to fund the repairs, some council members expressed concern that this is essentially asking residents to pay more for a problem that already exists. The goal of moving forward is to find a balance between robust defense against thieves and the logistical reality of maintaining thousands of lights across the city.

**Mill and Overlay (Road Surface Preservation)**
The committee reviewed the "Mill and Overlay" program, which involves milling off the top layer of asphalt and replacing it with a fresh coat to extend the life of the road. 

*   **Cost-Effectiveness:** This is highlighted as one of the city's most cost-effective methods for preserving pavement compared to full reconstruction.
*   **Operations & Equipment:** Public Works staff noted that while their current equipment works, investing in advanced rolling equipment (estimated at $200k–$300k) could cut work time in half and improve the quality of the finish. 
*   **Future Goals:** There is an active effort to get the city’s asphalt plant MnDOT certified so its products can be used more broadly across different projects.
