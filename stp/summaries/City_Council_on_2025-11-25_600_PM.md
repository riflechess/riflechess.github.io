#### Meeting Info
**Meeting Type:** City Council Truth in Taxation Hearing
**Date/Time:** November 25, 2025, 6:00 PM
**Purpose:** Review and discuss the proposed 2026 budget for the City of Saint Paul, focusing on truth-in-taxation and community input.

#### Summary

The meeting began with a brief introduction by Council President, followed by Chief Budget Officer Kamud Verma's presentation on the proposed 2026 budget. The proposed budget includes a $886 million operating budget, with a 3.9% increase in the general fund budget. However, property taxes are expected to rise by 5.3%, affecting residents and businesses.

The public hearing portion of the meeting allowed community members to share their thoughts on the proposed budget. Several speakers expressed concerns about rising property taxes, particularly for low-income individuals and families. Many spoke out against cuts to library hours, rec centers, and other essential services.

A significant focus was on the actions of the St. Paul Police Department during a recent ICE raid in the city. Speakers expressed outrage at the use of pepper spray and tear gas on protesters, as well as concerns about police collaboration with federal immigration authorities. Some speakers called for an audit of SPD spending and accountability measures to prevent future incidents.

Other topics discussed included:

* The impact of rising property taxes on small businesses and homeowners
* The need for increased funding for public services, such as libraries and rec centers
* Concerns about the safety of frontline workers, including library staff and rec center employees
* Calls for investment in housing programs and affordable housing initiatives

Council members acknowledged community concerns and promised to consider their input. However, the meeting concluded without a clear resolution or decision on the proposed budget.

**Notable Votes/Decisions:**

* The council did not take any votes or make decisions during the meeting.
* Council members acknowledged community concerns but promised to consider their input in future discussions.

**Controversies/Moments of Contention:**

* The use of pepper spray and tear gas on protesters during a recent ICE raid
* Police collaboration with federal immigration authorities
* Rising property taxes and their impact on low-income individuals and families
* Cuts to library hours, rec centers, and other essential services

**Key Issues Raised:**

* Property tax reform and relief for low-income residents
* Increased funding for public services, such as libraries and rec centers
* Safety concerns for frontline workers
* Community engagement and input in budget decision-making
