#### Meeting Info
**Meeting Type:** City Council Meeting
**Date/Time:** April 15, 2026, 3:30 PM
**Purpose:** Consideration of various resolutions and ordinances related to business signs, Dolores Huerta Day, fire chief qualifications, training, reporting, and programming on civil immigration laws, and other city matters.

#### Summary

* **Resolution 26-534 (Business Signs)**: The City Council adopted Resolution 26-534, initiating a study on the sign ordinance in St. Paul to make it easier for businesses to operate.
* **Dolores Huerta Day**: The City Council honored Dolores Huerta's legacy and recognized April 10th as Dolores Huerta Day in St. Paul. Guest speakers from Academia Cesar Chavez shared their appreciation for the resolution, highlighting Dolores Huerta's commitment to human dignity and her impact on the labor movement.
* **Fire Chief Qualifications**: The City Council approved Ordinance 26-17, establishing minimum qualifications for the Fire Chief position, including a selection committee that will make recommendations to the Mayor.
* **Public Hearings**: Several public hearings were held regarding various resolutions and ordinances, including:
	+ Resolution 26-617 (Dolores Huerta Day): The resolution was adopted unanimously after guest speakers shared their appreciation for Dolores Huerta's legacy.
	+ Ordinance 26-17 (Fire Chief Qualifications): The ordinance was approved with some discussion on the selection process for the Fire Chief position.
	+ Resolution 26-75 (Police Department Donations): The resolution was adopted to accept donations from the St. Paul Police Foundation and amend the grant budget.
	+ Resolution 26-76 (FEMA Grant Funding): The resolution was adopted to authorize acceptance of a FEMA grant for urban area preparedness and response.
* **Breakaway Music Festival**: A public hearing was held regarding Breakaway Music Festival's request for a sound level variance. After discussion, the City Council approved the resolution with some conditions, including the installation of sound blockers to minimize noise impact on nearby residents.
* **Legislative Hearing Consent Agenda**: The City Council considered several items from the legislative hearing consent agenda, including ratification of special tax assessments for property owners affected by emergency boardings.

No significant disagreements or contentious votes were reported.
