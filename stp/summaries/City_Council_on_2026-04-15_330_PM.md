#### Meeting Info

**Summary:**

The meeting began with a roll call and the Pledge of Allegiance. The consent agenda was approved without discussion. Resolution 26-534, Initiating Business Sign Zoning Code Study, was pulled from consent and passed unanimously.

Council Member Kim introduced Resolution 26-617, honoring the legacy of Dolores Huerta and recognizing April 10, 2026 as Dolores Huerta Day in St. Paul. The resolution acknowledged Huerta's contributions to the labor movement and her bravery in speaking out about sexual assault. Guest speakers Norma Garces and Katie Avenia from Academia Cesar Chavez spoke in support of the resolution.

The council then moved on to Item 30, final adoption of Ordinance 26-17, which establishes minimum qualifications for the Fire Chief position. Council Member Coleman introduced the motion, and after brief discussion, the ordinance was adopted unanimously.

Item 31, Ordinance 26-19, adding Chapter 44A to the Administrative Code to institute training, reporting, and programming related to the city's relationship with civil immigration laws and enforcement practices, was delayed due to a public hearing being reopened. The council voted to continue the public hearing until April 22nd.

The meeting also included final adoption of Ordinance 26-20, deleting Chapter 54 of the Legislative Code pertaining to rental application fees, which was passed unanimously.
