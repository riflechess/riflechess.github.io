#### Meeting Info
**Meeting Type:** City Council Meeting
**Date/Time:** 2026-04-15, 3:30 PM
**Purpose:** To discuss and vote on various resolutions and ordinances related to business sign zoning code study, tenant protections, public safety, and community events.

#### Summary

The meeting covered several topics related to business operations, public safety, and community events. The main decisions made during the meeting include:

* **Resolution 26-534**: Initiating a business sign zoning code study to make it easier for businesses to hang signs in downtown St. Paul and citywide.
	+ This resolution was pulled from the consent agenda and passed with a unanimous vote.
* **Resolution 26-617**: Honoring the legacy of Dolores Huerta and recognizing April 10, 2026 as Dolores Huerta Day in St. Paul.
	+ Council Member Kim introduced this resolution, which passed with a unanimous vote after brief remarks from Council Members Bowie and Yang.
* **Ordinance 26-17**: Establishing minimum qualifications for the Fire Chief position.
	+ This ordinance was adopted with a unanimous vote.
* **Public Hearing on Ordinance 26-19**: Reopening a public hearing to discuss a zoning code related to civil immigration laws and enforcement practices under Administrative Code, Chapter 44.
	+ The public hearing was reopened until April 22nd to allow for more time for transparency and input.
* **Resolution Public Hearings (Items 33-41)**: Various resolutions were adopted with unanimous votes, including authorizing the police department to accept donations from the St. Paul Police Foundation and accepting grant funds for local housing incentives accounts.

There was some discussion on the **Emergency Boarding Assessment** (Item 42) related to property at 665 Frye Street, where the property owner claimed they were not notified of the boarding and assessment process. Council Members Coleman and Yang expressed concerns about notification procedures and potential policy changes. After hearing from both testifiers, the resolution was adopted with a unanimous vote.

The meeting also included announcements from Vice President Yang and Council Members Coleman, Johnson, and Bowie regarding various community events and initiatives, including neighborhood cleanups, personal safety workshops, and library reopening updates.
