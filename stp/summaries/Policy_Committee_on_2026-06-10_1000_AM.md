#### Summary

**Prison Labor Reform & Abolishing "Slavery" Loopholes**
The committee heard from a coalition advocating for the end of what they described as a legal loophole allowing the exploitation of incarcerated individuals. 
*   **The Issue:** Currently, state laws allow private corporations to contract prison labor (often via MinCorp) for as little as 25 cents per hour. This creates an unfair advantage for corporations over local small businesses and unions who must pay fair wages.
*   **Community Impact:** The presentation highlighted that this system results in the theft of millions of dollars from low-income families and fails to provide a path for rehabilitation or reentry into the workforce. 
*   **Proposed Actions:** 
    *   A constitutional amendment to remove the "slavery" exception in the state's constitution (expected on the ballot in 2028).
    *   Legislation to reclassify incarcerated individuals as "workers" to mandate minimum wage and basic labor rights.
    *   **City Engagement:** Council members discussed how St. Paul can act as a leader by auditing city procurement contracts (specifically for furniture, laundry, and landscaping) to ensure the city isn't inadvertently funding these exploitative systems.

**Economic Development & Land Use Productivity**
A representative from Urban 3 presented an analysis of land-use economics specifically for St. Paul and Ramsey County.
*   **Tax Revenue Analysis:** The presentation revealed that St. Paul has a notably high percentage of tax-exempt property compared to other capital cities in the U.S., which impacts the city's ability to fund services from its existing land base. 
*   **Productivity Highlights:** Certain areas, particularly the Grand Avenue corridor and downtown mixed-use developments (like "The Times" block), were identified as highly productive zones for tax revenue.
*   **Recommendations for Growth:**
    *   **Compact Development:** The firm suggested moving toward more dense, multi-unit housing ("missing middle") to maximize the value of urban parcels.
    *   **Leveraging Transit:** There is a significant opportunity to leverage investments in transit (such as the Green Line and Snelling BRT) by simplifying the "entitlement" process for developers to build housing near station sites.
    *   **Public Assets:** The city was encouraged to look at underutilized public assets—like vacant lots or large parking areas—to transition into higher-value uses to bolster the local economy.
