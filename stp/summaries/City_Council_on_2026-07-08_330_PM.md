#### Meeting Info
**Type:** City Council Meeting
**Purpose:** Regular council proceedings, resolution approvals, and legislative hearings.
**Date/Time:** July 8, 2026, at 3:30 PM.

#### Summary
**Recognition of the "Kumbaya" Play & Juneteenth Arts**
The Council unanimously passed a resolution (7-0) to recognize and honor Rose McGee, the Sweet Potato Comfort Pie organization, and the Minnesota Humanities Center. This recognizes their 30 years of work using storytelling and art to preserve history and provide community education regarding Juneteenth.

**Tree Canopy Preservation Ordinance**
There was significant discussion regarding an ordinance aimed at protecting St. Paul’s tree canopy, particularly on the East Side. Council members highlighted that tree loss is a major factor in local climate action disparities. While there was broad support for the measure as a piece of "community-driven" legislation, the item was **laid over until July 15th** for final adoption rather than being passed immediately.

**Fire Certificate of Occupancy Appeal (683 Kansas Avenue)**
This item involved a high degree of frustration regarding property management and safety. A tenant at the property testified about significant issues including:
*   A lack of heat and hot water during the winter.
*   Leaking ceilings and electrical outlets that were overheating from the use of space heaters.
*   Missing smoke alarms and vent fans.

The tenant expressed frustration with the landlord’s failure to act on multiple orders since February. While the tenant's testimony was emotionally charged, the Council reached a **unanimous agreement** (7-0) to deny the appeal. This means the city will not waive its requirements; the property owner is still being ordered to complete all safety repairs by August 7th.

**Legislative Hearing Consent Agenda**
Items 40 through 49 were approved as part of the consent agenda, moving forward with the recommendations of the legislative hearing officer without further debate.

**Community News & Updates**
Several upcoming events and projects were highlighted for residents:
*   **Budget Awareness:** An "award budget" community conversation will be held on July 21st at the Hazel Park Recreation Center to discuss city priorities.
*   **Public Art:** The St. Paul Public Libraries is seeking artists to create a mural in the Hazel Heights Library.
*   **New Openings:** The Hamlin-Moodway Library officially opens on July 18th.
*   **Parks & Recreation:** A ribbon-cutting for the South St. Anthony Park took place today, and an ice cream social at the streetcar station is scheduled for this Friday.
*   **Housing Forum:** A housing forum hosted by SECO will be held on Monday nights at 6:30 PM (2105 Half Road).
