#### Summary
The council addressed several items ranging from personnel updates to major policy changes regarding the city's tree canopy and housing safety.

**Tree Preservation Ordinance (Item 39)**
This item received significant attention due to its impact on quality of life and the extensive amount of public testimony provided.
*   **The Discussion:** Residents and community leaders testified on a new ordinance focused on tree preservation for city-sponsored construction projects. A key point of discussion was the distinction between **preservation vs. replacement**. Speakers emphasized that once mature trees are lost, they cannot be replaced in our lifetime; therefore, construction plans must prioritize keeping existing trees over simply planting new ones as an "easy fix."
*   **Current Status:** This was a first reading and is laid over for further consideration on July 15. The council noted the lengthy process (over 14 months of public input) required to reach this point, and highlighted that the ordinance includes specific Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) for city staff to ensure it is enforced effectively rather than being a "feel-good" measure.

**Housing & Public Safety (Item 43)**
A hearing was held regarding **683 Kansas Avenue**, specifically concerning fire certificates of occupancy and property maintenance issues.
*   **The Conflict:** A tenant (the petitioner) testified about severe living conditions, including lack of heat/water during the winter and safety concerns with electrical equipment. The tenant expressed significant frustration with the landlord's failure to make repairs despite several notices from the city since February. 
*   **Resolution:** While the council acknowledged the tenant’s distress, they moved to deny the specific appeal but reaffirmed the requirement for the **property owner** to complete all necessary safety repairs by August 7th. The council aims to hold the property owners accountable for ensuring the unit meets fire and occupancy standards.
