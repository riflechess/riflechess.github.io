#### Meeting Info
**Type:** City Council Meeting  
**Purpose:** Legislative session and public hearings regarding city ordinances, resolutions, and local events.  
**Date/Time:** July 8, 2026, at 3:30 PM  
**Location:** Saint Paul City Hall

#### Summary
The council addressed a variety of items ranging from internal staffing updates to significant community-focused legislation regarding urban trees and public safety.

**Key Decisions & Resolutions**
*   **Staffing & Recognition:** The Council approved a technical update regarding the pay scale for GIS supervisors (Resolution 26-969). They also passed a resolution celebrating the 30th anniversary of "Kumbaya: The Juneteenth Story," honoring the artists and organizations involved in preserving local culture.
*   **Utility Extensions:** Initial readings were taken on ordinances to extend the current gas and electric franchise agreements with Xcel Energy through the end of this year. This provides more time for negotiations; these items will move to a public hearing on July 15th.
*   **Community Events:** The council approved sound level variances for two upcoming local events: the Rondo Block Party (August 1) and Chicano Fest (July 11).

**Public Safety & Quality of Life**
*   **Tree Preservation Ordinance (Item 39):** This item received significant attention during the public hearing. Residents and council members emphasized that this ordinance is specifically about *preservation*, not just replacement, as mature trees are vital infrastructure. The council noted that this ordinance took over a year to develop because of its importance to the city's canopy, particularly on the East Side. It will move to a final vote on July 15th.
*   **Fire Certificate Appeal (683 Kansas Ave):** A tenant testified regarding hazardous living conditions at a property with significant damage, including lack of heat and water for several months. While the tenant's specific appeal was denied, the Council moved to ensure that the **property owner** is held strictly accountable to complete all necessary repairs by August 7th to secure a certificate of occupancy.

**Community Updates & "Good News"**
The meeting concluded with several announcements regarding local neighborhood highlights:
*   A budget event will be held at the Hazel Park Recreation Center on July 21st.
*   A call for artists is open for a mural at the newly renovated Hamilton Heights Library (note: some residents may refer to it as Hamlin Midway).
*   New park celebrations and local community events, such as an ice cream social in Ward 5 and "AI Magic Night" downtown, were announced by various council members.
