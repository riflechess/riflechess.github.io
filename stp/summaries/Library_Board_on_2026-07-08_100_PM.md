#### Meeting Info
**Type:** Library Board Meeting  
**Purpose:** Presentation on Equity, Inclusion, and Anti-Racism initiatives at St. Paul Public Library (SPPL).  
**Date/Time:** July 8th, 1:00 PM  
**Context:** Discussion of internal organizational structures, staff training, and community-facing programs regarding diversity and inclusion.

#### Summary
The meeting focused on the multi-layered work being done by St. Paul Public Library to foster equity and anti-racism. Key updates included:

*   **Internal Initiatives & Staff Development:** 
    *   The library has established a "Managers of Color Cohort" to provide specialized peer learning, support, and skill-building for management staff from diverse backgrounds.
    *   A new **Staff Well-being Initiative** is in its second phase, aiming to create sustainable habits to mitigate the impact of public service on employee well-being.
    *   The **Equity Change Team** operates as a vehicle for staff to lead projects, such as implementing "chosen and preferred names" in the patron system (completed in early 2024).

*   **Community & Cultural Programming:**
    *   **Community Competencies:** Training programs were implemented to educate library staff on the histories and cultures of the diverse communities they serve. This included specific training led by experts from the Minnesota Historical Society and indigenous educators.
    *   **Inclusion Events:** A 2024 initiative for Trans Day of Visibility included printmaking activities and special displays at the Arlington Hills and Rice Street libraries.

*   **Council Discussions & Points of Inquiry:**
    *   A significant portion of the meeting involved a dialogue with Council Member Kim regarding how "white supremacy" manifests in daily operations. Specifically, the discussion focused on how **urgency** in professional environments can sometimes lead to shortcuts that bypass necessary relationship-building or comprehensive inclusivity. 
    *   Council Member Noecker inquired about the overlap between library-specific equity work and broader city-wide initiatives. It was noted that while many programs are city-wide, some must be tailored specifically for library staff due to their unique public-facing schedules.
    *   Council Member Ng highlighted the importance of these roles amidst a national trend of cutting diversity programs, emphasizing the need for clear paths for frontline workers from marginalized communities to move into management roles.

*   **Upcoming Community Events:**
    *   The **Hamlin Midway Library** is scheduled to open on **Saturday, July 18th**, with festivities including a ribbon-cutting at 11:15 AM, tours, and community celebrations.
