#### Meeting Info
**Meeting Type:** Public Safety Committee
**Purpose:** To discuss updates on Operation Metro Surge, its impact on the community, and potential legal actions against federal agents.
**Date/Time:** 2026-03-25 10:00 AM
**Speakers:**
* Lindsey Middlecamp (Minnesota Attorney General's Office)
* John Beeler (Policy Counsel, ACLU Minnesota)

#### Summary

The Public Safety Committee meeting discussed updates on Operation Metro Surge and its impact on the community. Lindsey Middlecamp from the Minnesota Attorney General's Office presented on their role in investigating federal actions and potential litigation against federal agents. The AGO has been receiving reports of constitutional violations through their portal and has referred some cases to private attorneys.

John Beeler from the ACLU Minnesota discussed ongoing lawsuits against federal agents, including First Amendment and Fourth Amendment violations. He highlighted the need for state-level remedies due to a gap in federal law that allows individuals to sue local governments but not the federal government for constitutional rights violations.

County Attorney John Choi and Chief of Staff Maria Sarabia from Ramsey County provided updates on their office's response to Operation Metro Surge, including creating a public lawyers' group with other cities and counties to address immigration enforcement issues. They also discussed policy changes and system changes made in response to the surge, such as updating guidance for law enforcement agencies and creating a new memo on reporting crime.

Ramsey County has taken steps to support residents affected by the surge, including providing signage, training staff, and connecting with partners. The county has also instituted a temporary relief for property tax payments due to increased financial strain on residents.

The committee discussed potential legislation related to Operation Metro Surge, including a bill to close the loophole that allows individuals to sue local governments but not the federal government for constitutional rights violations.
