#### Meeting Info
**Meeting Type:** Regular City Council Meeting
**Date/Time:** November 19, 2025, 3:30 PM
**Location:** St. Paul City Hall
**Attendees:** City Council Members (Kim, Yang, Bowie, Coleman, Johnson, Jost), City President Noecker, Staff Members

#### Summary
The meeting covered various agenda items, including:

* Resolutions and Ordinances:
	+ Resolution 25-1752: Directing the Department of Safety and Inspections to revoke a liquor license due to non-compliance.
	+ Resolution 25-1781: Approving the Mayor's appointment of Council Member Yang to the St. Paul Port Authority Board of Commissioners.
	+ Resolution 25-1782: Approving the Mayor's appointment of Council Member Yang to the Board of Water Commissioners.
	+ Ordinance 25-63: Amending the Legislative Code Chapter 220 to allow for garbage cart sharing at properties with two or more dwelling units.
	+ Ordinance 25-66: Creating regulations for registration of cannabis and hemp businesses, permit requirements, and process for temporary cannabis events.
* Public Hearing:
	+ Ordinance 25-72: Amending chapters 60, 61, 68, and 72 of the Legislative Code pertaining to the Mississippi River Corridor critical area and floodplain management overlay districts.
	+ The public hearing was continued until December 3rd for final adoption or introduction of amendments.
* Port Authority Budget Presentation:
	+ Todd Hurley, President and CEO of the St. Paul Port Authority, presented a budget overview and discussed various projects, including redeveloping underused land in the city and managing the St. Paul Harbor.
* Legislative Hearing Consent Agenda Items 32-45 were approved as amended.

Notable discussions and decisions:

* Council Member Bowie expressed concerns about the proposed MRCCA ordinance, particularly with regards to height restrictions and conditional use permits.
* Council Member Yang emphasized the importance of bird-safe glass and suggested that the study on bird-safe glass be expanded to cover areas outside of the MRCA district.
* The City President and other council members discussed the importance of community engagement and transparency in the decision-making process.
* A resolution was adopted authorizing the city through its fire department to amend a joint powers agreement with the state of Minnesota for a hazardous materials response team and amending the 2025 financing and spending plans for the fire department.

The meeting concluded with the approval of several resolutions, ordinances, and other business items.
