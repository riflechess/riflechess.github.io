#### Meeting Info
* **Meeting Type:** Library Board Meeting
* **Purpose:** To discuss and review library-related matters, including e-book legislation and fundraising campaigns.
* **Date/Time:** May 6th, 2026 at 1:00 PM
* **Attendees:** Library Board Members, Director Hartman, Sherry Wichichu (MELSA Executive Director), Council Member Kim, Council President Noecker, and other library staff.

#### Summary

The meeting began with the approval of the minutes from the previous meeting. The next item on the agenda was Resolution 26-621, which authorized the St. Paul Public Library to accept a $500 gift from the Science Across America program. The resolution was approved unanimously.

Sherry Wichichu, MELSA Executive Director, presented an update on e-book legislation in Minnesota and across the country. She discussed the challenges of accessing e-books due to restrictive library contracts and the need for legislation to promote fair pricing models and expand access to e-books. Council Member Coleman asked about litigation related to the Maryland law, which was found unconstitutional, but Wichichu explained that it was not appealed and instead led to a reevaluation of the approach.

Council President Noecker expressed concern about the trigger laws in some states, including Minnesota, which require a population threshold before the legislation can take effect. Council Member Coleman suggested exploring alternative approaches, such as grant programs or blackout periods, if the legislation is unsuccessful.

The meeting also featured a presentation by Director Hartman and Senior Director of Development for the Friends of the St. Paul Public Library on their successful Books for All campaign. The campaign raised over $70,000 in a short period to support library collections and demonstrated a strong partnership between the Friends and the library.

Council Member Yang praised the campaign's success and expressed appreciation for the enthusiasm and dedication of the Friends and library staff. Council Member Johnson commended the team for telling a compelling story and reaching across the city with the fundraising effort.

The meeting concluded with no further business to come before the Board, and the adjournment was announced.
