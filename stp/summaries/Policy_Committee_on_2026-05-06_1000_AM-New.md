#### Meeting Info
* Type: City Council meeting
* Purpose: Policy Committee discussion on rooming house definition and Ramsey County's Building Stronger Together initiative
* Date/Time: 2026-05-06, 10:00 AM
* Attendees: City Council members, Ramsey County representatives (Josh Olson, Carrie Collins, County Manager Becker), Planning Director

#### Summary

**Policy Committee Discussion on Rooming House Definition**

The Policy Committee met to discuss the rooming house definition in Saint Paul's zoning code. Katie Dadliss from the Planning and Economic Development department presented an update on the definition, highlighting inconsistencies between the zoning code and housing code definitions. The discussion centered around updating the definition to reflect current needs and practices.

Council Member Coleman suggested that the definition should focus on rental arrangements rather than dwelling units, while Council Member Johnson noted that individual leases for rooming units are a key characteristic of SROs. Vice President Yang expressed support for updating the definition to make it more inclusive and welcoming of various housing types.

The committee agreed to move forward with updating the definition and asked PED staff to explore options for modernizing the language.

**Ramsey County's Building Stronger Together Initiative**

County Manager Becker presented Ramsey County's Building Stronger Together initiative, a multi-year investment framework aimed at strengthening downtown Saint Paul and the county's economic development efforts. The plan focuses on two areas: supporting a dynamic downtown and strengthening economic development by selling county-owned land.

The committee discussed the initiative's goals and strategies, including creating signature public spaces, connecting to the Mississippi River, and enhancing multimodal transportation. County Manager Becker highlighted the importance of building a strong tax base and leveraging the county's assets to drive growth.

**EDA (Economic Development Authority) Programming**

Josh Olson from Ramsey County presented an update on EDA programming, which will support businesses in Saint Paul through technical assistance, direct business support, and investment in affordable housing. The committee discussed the potential for EDA programming to address issues such as rent relief for businesses and temporary solutions for pedestrian safety.

**City-County Partnership**

Council Member Johnson emphasized the importance of effective city-county partnership in implementing EDA programs, highlighting the need for clear communication and joint powers agreements to facilitate resource sharing between entities.

Council Member Lee praised the county's strong partnerships with the sheriff's department and expressed support for incorporating public safety initiatives into the Building Stronger Together framework.
