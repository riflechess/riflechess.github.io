#### Meeting Info
**Type:** City Council Meeting
**Purpose:** Regular Meeting of the Saint Paul City Council on May 13th, 2026
**Date/Time:** Thursday, May 13th, 2026 at 3:30 PM
**Context:** Councilmember Kim participated remotely.

#### Summary

The meeting began with a roll call vote to adopt the consent agenda, which passed unanimously. The council then moved on to discuss Item 15, Resolution 26-791, which approves Melanie McMahon's appointment as Director of Planning and Economic Development. Council Member Johnson motioned for a two-week layover to discuss the partnership between the city and director, citing concerns about communication and deliverables. After discussion from various council members, the vote was taken, with seven in favor (Kim, Yang, Bowie, Coleman, Jost, Noecker) and none opposed, laying over the resolution until May 27th.

The next item was a public hearing on Resolution Public Hearing 26-105, which approved the petition to vacate the public right-of-way Morton Street in Baker Playground. The council then moved on to Item 18, Resolution 26-532, which proposed adverse action against the A&M Bar and Grill for violating liquor laws after a shooting incident occurred at the bar. The city attorney's office presented evidence of the violations, including security footage showing the metal detector not being used properly. After some discussion and clarification from both parties, Council President Noecker suggested pausing the item to allow further discussion between Ms. Schaefer (city attorney) and Mr. Rivera (bar owner).

The council then moved on to Item 19, Ordinance 26-25, which amended Chapter 66 of the Legislative Code regarding lot coverage for providing functional green roof space. The ordinance passed with a unanimous vote. Council Member Coleman also provided an update on Operation Metro Surge, which led to the adoption of Ordinance 26-26, updating Chapter 310 of the Legislative Code pertaining to uniform license procedures.

The council then proceeded to discuss various public hearings, including items related to tree planting programs and sound level variances for amplified music events. After some discussion and votes, all items passed unanimously.

Finally, Council Member Johnson provided news from her ward, announcing the Hamsbury redevelopment update event on May 18th and a town hall meeting on election protection on May 19th. Other council members also shared upcoming community events and news. The meeting concluded with a vote to adjourn.
