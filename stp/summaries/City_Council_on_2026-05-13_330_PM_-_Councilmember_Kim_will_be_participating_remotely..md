#### Meeting Info
**Meeting Type:** City Council Meeting
**Purpose:** To discuss various city business, including appointments, licenses, and community events
**Date/Time:** May 13, 2026, 3:30 PM
**Attendees:** City Council Members Kim, Yang, Bowie, Coleman, Johnson, Jost, President Noecker, and other officials

#### Summary

The meeting began with a roll call vote on the consent agenda, which was approved without opposition. The council then discussed and voted on various items, including:

* Resolution 26-791: Approving the mayor's appointment of Melanie McMahon as Director of Planning and Economic Development (PED). After discussion, a motion to lay over the item for two weeks was passed with six votes in favor and one opposed.
* Ordinance 26-25: Amending Chapter 66 of the Legislative Code to correct a Scrivener's Error related to allowing additional lot coverage for providing functional green roof space. The ordinance was adopted without opposition.
* Resolution Public Hearing 26-105: Approving the petition of the Department of Parks and Rec on behalf of the City of St. Paul to vacate the public right-of-way Morton Street in Baker Playground. The resolution was approved without opposition.
* Resolution Public Hearing 26-116: Authorizing the police department to accept a donation from the Ramsey County Chiefs of Police Association for officer travel costs during National Police Week in Washington, D.C. The resolution was adopted without opposition.

The council also discussed and voted on items related to business licenses, including:

* Item 18: Adverse action against A&M Bar and Grill (DBA St. Paul Saloon) for violating city code and license conditions. After a discussion and clarification of the facts, the item was laid over pending further discussion between the licensee and the Department of Safety and Inspections.

Additionally, council members shared news from their wards, including:

* Ms. Coleman announced the Hamlin Midway Coalition's Mighty Midway Festival event on May 16th.
* Vice President Yang mentioned the Elders Lodge's Spring Swing event on May 21st.
* Ms. Johnson shared a Hamsbury redevelopment update on May 18th and an upcoming town hall meeting on election protection on May 19th.
* Ms. Kim announced Bryce Lerpender's hosting of Bloom this Saturday at McCarran's.
* Ms. Bui mentioned the Como Community Garage Sale from May 14-17, the state legislature's approval of $40 million in rental assistance, and the St. Paul Summer Choice Fair on May 16th.

The meeting concluded with a discussion of the Tenant Protections Ordinance going into effect tomorrow and the city's partnership with multiple departments to ensure its successful implementation.
