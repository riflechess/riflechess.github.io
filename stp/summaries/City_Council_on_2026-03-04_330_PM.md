#### Summary
The city council met to discuss several items including a resolution related to the vacant building fee. The council approved a resolution that would extend the pre-eviction filing notice from 30 days to 60 days for non-payment of rent, giving families more time to access rental assistance and mutual aid resources.

The council also discussed an ordinance granting authority to the Port Authority to issue and sell bonds in the principal amount of $6 million. The ordinance was approved with a unanimous vote.

Additionally, the council reviewed several zoning applications, including one related to re-zoning property at 325 Commercial Street from H1 residential to I1 light industrial. The council voted to continue the public hearing on this item until March 11th.

The council also heard from various staff members and community members regarding different items on the agenda, including a presentation by Metro Transit staff on the Bronze Line project. The council approved several resolutions related to transportation projects, including one authorizing the execution of a grant agreement for the benefit of Face to Face at 1170 North Arcade Street.

The council also discussed an appeal of a decision by the Board of Zoning Appeals regarding a variance request to convert an existing lit freestanding sign to a dynamic display at 1305 7th Street West. The council voted to approve the appeal, finding that the applicant had demonstrated practical difficulties in complying with the provision.

Finally, the council discussed several legislative hearing items, including one related to a vacant building fee. The council approved an amended recommendation to require the assessment be paid over a period of five years.
