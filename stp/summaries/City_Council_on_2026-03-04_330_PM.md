#### Meeting Info
* Type: City Council Meeting
* Purpose: To discuss and vote on various city ordinances, resolutions, and other business related to the City of Saint Paul
* Date/Time: 2026-03-04, 3:30 PM
* Other Relevant Contextual Information: This meeting features a special thank you to outgoing Council Vice President Kim, discussion of a resolution to temporarily extend pre-eviction filing notice from 30 days to 60 days for immigrant families affected by Operation Metro Surge, and the final adoption of several ordinances related to zoning, transportation network companies, and grant funds.

#### Summary
* The meeting began with a special thank you to outgoing Council Vice President Kim, who was praised for her leadership and commitment to listening to all council members.
* The council then voted to adopt ordinance 26-9, which grants the Heights Community Energy, Inc. a geothermal energy franchise.
* Next, the council voted to adopt ordinance 26-11, which authorizes the port authority to issue and sell bonds in the principal amount of $6 million for the redevelopment of part of the St. Joseph's Hospital site.
* The council then considered and adopted ordinance 26-16, which amends chapter 65 and 66 of the legislative code pertaining to drive-through sales and services and walk-up service windows.
* Additionally, the council voted to adopt a resolution supporting the bronze line locally preferred alternative to the Metropolitan Council in Ramsey County.
* In a public hearing, the council considered an appeal of a decision by the Board of Zoning Appeals (BZA) denying a variance request to convert an existing lit freestanding sign to a dynamic display at 1305 7th Street West. The motion to approve the appeal was passed with seven votes in favor.
* The council also held a public hearing on grievances about federal immigration operations, which was continued until March 11th.
* Several legislative hearing items were considered, including a request for an additional vacant building fee at 1339 Van Buren Avenue North. After testimony from property owner Beth Jackson, the motion to approve the legislative hearing officer's recommendation was passed with seven votes in favor.
* The meeting concluded with news from the wards, including updates on construction projects and upcoming events in Saint Paul.
