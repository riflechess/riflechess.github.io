#### Meeting Info
**Type:** City Council Meeting  
**Purpose:** Regular business meeting and legislative hearing regarding city ordinances, resolutions, and zoning appeals.  
**Date/Time:** July 22, 2026, at 3:30 PM

#### Summary

**Public Health and Community Recognition**
*   **Community Health Workers Day:** The Council unanimously approved a resolution to recognize July 28, 2026, as Community Health Workers Day. This initiative aims to highlight the role of these workers in bridging gaps in healthcare access and promoting equity across various cultures and communities in St. Paul.

**Housing and Land Use (Zoning Appeals)**
*   **418 Sherburn Avenue:** The Council heard an appeal for a variance to establish a 24-resident supportive housing facility. The request was denied (7-0). Staff and the City Attorney noted that the petition was largely driven by funding considerations rather than unique property issues, and the site remains too close to other existing facilities to grant the variance under current rules.
*   **519 Farrington Street:** A similar appeal for an 11-resident supportive housing facility was heard. The Council voted unanimously (7-0) to deny the request based on findings that it would create a "cluster" of high-intensity usage and did not meet specific hardship criteria.

**Public Safety & Infrastructure Funding**
*   **Emergency Shelter Facilities:** A joint powers agreement with Ramsey County was approved to manage approximately $1.08 million in local affordable housing funds to support the operation of emergency shelter facilities.
*   **Infrastructure Projects:** The Council approved funding and spending plans for two major construction projects:
    *   The Wabasha Bridge Rehabilitation Project.
    *   The University Avenue Street Reconstruction Project.

**City Operations & Personnel**
*   **Deputy City Clerk Role:** A first reading was held to establish a formal, unclassified role for the Deputy City Clerk. This is intended to ensure seamless legal authority and better compensation for the position in cases of absence or turnover. 
*   **Youth Minimum Wage:** The Council began a review (first reading) of an ordinance to eliminate the "90-day youth wage" exception. If passed, workers aged 14–17 would be required to earn the full city minimum wage immediately upon hire, rather than a lower rate for the first three months.

**Business and Utility Updates**
*   **Franchise Agreements:** The Council adopted two ordinances regarding Northern States Power (DBA XL Energy) concerning electric and gas franchise rights.
*   **Library Lease:** The City approved a resolution to renew its lease with Metropolitan State University for the Dayton's Bluff Library.

**Quality of Life & Local Permitting**
*   **Noise Variances:** The Council approved sound-level variances for two upcoming local events: one at the MLK Rec Center (August 4) and another at Dual Citizen Brewing (August 7–8).
*   **Nuisance Abatement:** Regarding a vehicle removal order on Wyoming Street East, the Council granted a three-week extension to the owner to clear the property.
