#### Meeting Info
* **Meeting Type:** Budget Committee
* **Date/Time:** 2026-04-22 10:00 AM
* **Purpose:** Review of City Special Fund FTEs and General Fund Budget to Actuals for Quarter 1 of 2026
* **Attendees:** Chair Johnson, Council Members Bowie, Coleman, Jost, Kim, Noecker, Yang; Vice President Noecker; Deputy Director Logsdon (OFS); Director Harney (new OFS Director)

#### Summary

The Budget Committee meeting covered the City Special Fund FTEs and General Fund Budget to Actuals for Quarter 1 of 2026. Deputy Director Logsdon presented information on special fund FTEs, highlighting that 780.36 FTEs are funded in special funds, representing $97.5 million in salary and fringe benefits. The majority (89%) of these FTEs have ongoing funding sources, while 11% do not.

The Committee also reviewed the General Fund Budget to Actuals for Quarter 1 of 2026, with Deputy Director Logsdon noting that spending is similar to last year at this time. Council President Aker requested a budget year-to-date column to compare actual spending to expected spending. The mayor's office has overspent due to severance payments and vacation payouts from the previous administration.

The Committee also discussed revenue, noting that Q1 revenues are lower than spending due to timing issues with major revenue sources (property tax and local government aid). Council Member Coleman asked about the impact of Ramsey County's delayed property tax payment program on the city's budget. Deputy Director Logsdon stated that the city will still receive a payment from the county at the end of May.

Council Members Kim, Joost, and Noecker discussed the concept of a "minimum viable product" for the city, which refers to the minimum level of services and funding required to maintain basic city functions. They also touched on the idea of creating a baseline or minimum levy that accounts for inflation, contracts, and other costs.

The Committee agreed to schedule presentations from external partners to provide independent analysis and fresh perspectives on the budget. Council Member Bowie suggested seeking external analysis on income levels, earning potential, and pain points in neighborhoods. She also requested tracking top revenue generators and investing more into high-revenue departments or programs.

No further action was taken as this was the first meeting of the Budget Committee for 2026. The meeting adjourned with a discussion to continue at the next meeting.
