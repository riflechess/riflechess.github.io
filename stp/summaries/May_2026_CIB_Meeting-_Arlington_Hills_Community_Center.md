#### Meeting Info
**Type:** Committee of the Whole (COW) meeting
**Purpose:** Discussion and potential action on various city projects, issues, and proposals
**Date/Time:** May 2026
**Location:** Arlington Hills Community Center

#### Summary
The meeting began with a presentation from the Public Works department regarding the upcoming paving project for several streets in the city. The plan includes resurfacing multiple roads, improving bike lanes, and enhancing pedestrian safety.

A key point of discussion was the proposal to allocate funds for additional community policing initiatives. While some committee members expressed support for increased funding, others raised concerns about the potential impact on the city's budget. After a brief discussion, a motion was made to approve $200,000 for the initiative, which passed with a vote of 7-2.

A separate proposal aimed at increasing recycling rates through education and outreach programs received unanimous approval, with an allocated budget of $150,000.

Some concerns were raised regarding potential noise disturbances from the new development on University Avenue. The committee agreed to direct staff to work with the developers to find solutions that minimize disruptions for nearby residents.

Lastly, the committee discussed a proposal to host a city-wide festival in June 2026, which includes a budget request of $50,000. While some members showed enthusiasm for the event, others expressed reservations about the costs and potential logistical challenges.

The meeting concluded with no further action items or upcoming events announced.
