#### Meeting Info
**Meeting:** Housing Redevelopment Authority (HRA) meeting on March 25, 2026
**Date/Time:** 2:00 PM
**Purpose:** Discussion and approval of various resolutions related to commercial corridor programs, down payment assistance program guidelines, business assistance fund guidelines, and loan agreements.

#### Summary

The meeting began with the discussion of Resolution 26-405, which approved the commercial corridor program guidelines citywide. Version two of the guidelines was adopted as amended, including changes to the Rice Street Alliance host organization to the North End neighborhood organization. The resolution aims to encourage investment in commercial corridors across the city and promote economic development.

The next item discussed was Resolution RES 26-407, which amended the down payment assistance program guidelines citywide. Version two of the guidelines included renters in the program and made changes to ensure historical accuracy regarding displacement during urban renewal projects. Commissioner Bowie highlighted the importance of including Rondo Avenue Street and St. Anthony Avenue in the program's boundaries.

Resolution 26-409 was also approved, which updated the guidelines for the HRA Business Assistance Fund. The resolution includes a $5 million investment in downtown Saint Paul, with $3 million dedicated to converting underutilized office space and $2 million for activating retail and vacant storefronts.

The final item discussed was the staff report on authorization of CDBG loan agreement by and between the City of St. Paul and JRDM Investments, LLC for 202 Cesar Chavez Street. The project will provide a new use for the space and create three full-time jobs through year five. Commissioner Ecker expressed concern about supporting businesses that have previously closed.

The meeting concluded with a discussion on Resolution SR26-54, which sought authorization to designate Reuter Walton Development as a tentative developer of the Fox Lot District 3, Ward 2. The proposal includes building 300 units of affordable housing on HRA-owned land and includes a mix of 81 two-bedroom, 157 three-bedroom, and 62 four-bedroom units.

Overall, the meeting focused on promoting economic development, investing in commercial corridors, and providing affordable housing opportunities across the city.
