#### Summary
The May MACPD meeting covered several topics related to community development and city policies.

*   **West Side Community Development**: A presentation on the proposed affordable housing project in the area was met with enthusiasm from attendees. However, some concerns were raised regarding parking and traffic congestion.
*   **Resolution for Public Safety Funding**: The group discussed a resolution calling for increased funding for public safety initiatives. Some members argued that more should be done to address gun violence in the community.
    *   One attendee proposed redirecting funds from other budget lines to support public safety efforts, but this proposal was met with skepticism by others who felt it would disproportionately affect essential services.
*   **Update on Local Businesses**: A representative from the local business association reported that several new businesses are opening in the area, including a community-owned grocery store and a coworking space.
    *   Some attendees expressed concerns about gentrification and whether these new developments will make it difficult for long-time residents to afford housing.

The meeting concluded with a decision to support the public safety funding resolution, but not without significant discussion on the proposal to redirect funds. The group also agreed to work together to ensure that community development initiatives align with the needs of local residents.
