#### Meeting Info

* **Location:** City Council Chambers, Saint Paul, MN

#### Summary

The meeting began with a consent agenda, where items 2-6 were approved without discussion. Item 5 was pulled for separate consideration and amended to correct a technical error regarding notice requirements.

Resolution 26-535 was introduced by Council Member Bowie, declaring April 7th as National Black Bookstore Day. The resolution acknowledged the importance of Black-owned bookstores in preserving cultural heritage, promoting literacy, and fostering community power. Dr. Artika Tyner, founder of Planting People, Growing Justice Bookstore, spoke in support of the resolution, highlighting the significance of Black bookstores in shaping the country's culture and economy.

The resolution was adopted with seven votes in favor and none opposed. Council Members Johnson, Kim, and Bowie commended Dr. Tyner for her leadership and acknowledged the importance of recognizing National Black Bookstore Day.

Following the adoption of Resolution 26-535, the meeting moved on to public hearings for various ordinances and resolutions. The main discussion points revolved around Ordinance 26-19, which aimed to provide training, reporting, and programming related to civil immigration laws and enforcement practices. Several speakers, including Kathy Quick, Laurel St. Marie, Jerusha, Reverend Karen Herring, and Jill Toll, spoke in favor of the ordinance, highlighting its importance in promoting accountability and transparency within the police department.

The meeting also covered other ordinances and resolutions, including:

* Ordinance 26-20: Deleting Chapter 54 of the Legislative Code pertaining to rental application fees
* Ordinance 26-21: Updating Chapter 13 of the Administrative Code pertaining to Department of Safety and Inspections
* Resolution Public Hearing 26-34: Accepting grant funds for a Metropolitan Council LTB. TBRA Grant Program
* Resolution Public Hearing 26-35: Accepting grant funds for a Metropolitan Council LCA seed grant

Council Members also shared news from their wards, including the spring holiday carnival, Trans Day of Visibility, and local events such as the Black Authors Expo and Hallie Q Brown's 97-year anniversary celebration.

The meeting concluded with no further discussion or action items.
