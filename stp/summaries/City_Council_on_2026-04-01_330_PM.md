#### Meeting Info
**Meeting Type:** City Council Meeting
**Purpose:** To discuss various resolutions and ordinances, public hearings, and updates from the wards
**Date/Time:** April 1, 2026, 3:30 PM
**Location:** Saint Paul City Hall

#### Summary
The meeting began with the consent agenda, where items 2-6 were approved as amended. Item 5, a resolution approving a liquor license application for Luxe Event Planning and Decor LLC, was pulled for separate consideration and passed after a motion to approve version 2 of the resolution.

Next, Council Member Bowie introduced Resolution 26-535, declaring April 7th as National Black Bookstore Day. The resolution acknowledged the significance of Black-owned bookstores in preserving knowledge, promoting literacy, and fostering community dialogue. After a heartfelt introduction by Dr. Artika Tyner, the founder of Planting People Growing Justice, the resolution was adopted with a vote of 7-0.

The public hearing section of the agenda included several items, including Ordinance 26-17, which establishes the minimum qualifications for the position of Fire Chief. The public hearing on this item was continued until April 15th at the request of Council Member Coleman. Kathy Quick, a resident and member of Unidos Minnesota's organizing team, spoke in favor of the ordinance, highlighting its importance in providing training and accountability measures to prevent police involvement in federal immigration activities.

Other public hearings included Ordinance 26-19, which adds Chapter 44A to the administrative code to institute training and reporting related to civil immigration laws and enforcement practices. Several residents testified in support of this ordinance, sharing their experiences with ICE raids and excessive use of force by St. Paul Police Department officers.

The council also heard public hearings on Ordinance 26-20, deleting Chapter 54 of the Legislative Code pertaining to rental application fees, and Resolution Public Hearing 26-34, accepting grant funds for a Metropolitan Council LTB.TBRA Grant Program.

In other news from the wards, Vice President Yang announced the spring holiday carnival happening this Saturday at the Arlington Hills Community Center. Council Member Kim shared her support for trans community members and condemned the Supreme Court's decision to strike down Colorado's ban on conversion therapy. Council Member Bowie highlighted two events in Ward 1: the Black Authors Expo and the Hallie Q Brown 97-year anniversary celebration.

Council Member Johnson announced that rec centers would be offering all-day programming during spring break, as well as a prom dress sale at Hazel Park Rec Center. Finally, she reminded residents about the Emergency Rental Assistance Program, which has reopened for applications from St. Paul residents.
