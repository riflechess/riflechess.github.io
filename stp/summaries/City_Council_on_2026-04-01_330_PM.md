#### Meeting Info
* **Meeting Type:** Saint Paul City Council meeting
* **Date/Time:** April 1, 2026, 3:30 PM
* **Location:** Saint Paul City Hall
* **Agenda Items:** Consent agenda items 2-6, Resolution 26-505, Resolution 26-535, Ordinance 26-17 (Administrative Ordinance Establishing the Minimum Qualifications of Vacancy for the Position of Fire Chief), and several public hearing items

#### Summary

The Saint Paul City Council meeting covered various topics. The consent agenda was approved with some minor modifications. 

Resolution 26-505, approving a liquor on-sale license application from Luxe Event Planning and Decor LLC (doing business as Ivory) for its new location at 12593 East, Unit 127, was adopted after a technical correction was made.

The council declared April 7th as National Black Bookstore Day in recognition of the importance of black-owned bookstores and their contributions to community education, activism, cultural expression, civic dialogue, and economic empowerment. Dr. Artika Tyner, founder of Planting People Growing Justice Bookstore, spoke about the significance of this day and her work.

The council then moved on to public hearings for Ordinance 26-17 (Administrative Ordinance Establishing the Minimum Qualifications of Vacancy for the Position of Fire Chief) and several other items. Kathy Quick testified in favor of Ordinance 26-19, adding chapter 44A to the administrative code to institute training, reporting, and programming related to the city's relationship with civil immigration laws and enforcement practices under administrative code chapter 44. She expressed concerns about the trust between law enforcement and communities, citing instances where St. Paul police engaged in excessive use of force against peaceful crowds.

The council approved several resolutions for grant funding and amendments to the operating budget for various projects, including a Metropolitan Council LTB. TBRA Grant Program for Ham's Brewery West End and a seed grant for Rondo Revitalize at 708 Marshall Avenue.

Finally, council members shared news from their wards, highlighting events such as the spring holiday carnival in Arlington Hills Community Center and the Black Authors Expo sponsored by the St. Paul Public Schools.

The meeting concluded with the announcement of the Emergency Rental Assistance Program's reopening and details on how to apply for assistance.
