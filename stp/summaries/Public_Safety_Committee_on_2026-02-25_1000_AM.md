#### Summary

The Public Safety Committee meeting focused on the role and function of the Peace Officers Standards and Training (POST) board. Eric Missel, executive director of POST, attended to discuss the board's standards, process, and procedures for educating the council about their jurisdiction and role.

Key takeaways from the meeting include:

* The POST board is a licensing regulatory agency responsible for overseeing pre-service education, testing, licensing, continuing education, and violations of standard conduct and licensure among peace officers.
* There are 17 members appointed by the governor to serve on the POST board, with four-year staggered terms.
* The board has jurisdiction over misconduct complaints against peace officers in Minnesota, including cases involving domestic violence, use of force, and other egregious behavior.
* The POST board receives complaints through various channels, including online reporting systems and referrals from law enforcement agencies.
* Complaints must be specific and identify the standard of conduct allegedly violated by the officer.
* If a complaint is dismissed, the complainant may not have access to information about the outcome.

Notable points of contention or disagreement:

* Council member Johnson raised concerns about the POST board's jurisdiction over misconduct complaints, citing instances where internal agency policies were not enforced.
* Director Missel clarified that the POST board does not have jurisdiction over internal agency policies, but rather focuses on licensing and regulatory issues.
* The committee discussed the increasing number of complaints filed against peace officers in Minnesota since 2021, with some members expressing concerns about potential bias or profiling.

The meeting concluded with a vote to adjourn, with plans for future meetings to be held every fourth Wednesday at 10:00 AM.
