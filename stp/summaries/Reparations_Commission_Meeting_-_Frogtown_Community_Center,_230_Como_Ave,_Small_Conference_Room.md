#### Summary

The Reparations Commission meeting was held to introduce the concept of reparations for historical injustices faced by communities of color in Saint Paul. The commission aims to identify and address systemic inequalities that have led to disparities in education, employment, healthcare, and housing.

Key decisions:

* Establishment of a research team to investigate the history of redlining and other discriminatory practices in Saint Paul
* Creation of a community engagement process to gather input from affected communities on reparations efforts
* Allocation of $100,000 from the city's budget for initial research and planning

Some attendees expressed concerns about the feasibility and effectiveness of reparations efforts, citing potential resistance from some community members. Commissioner Maria Hernandez emphasized that the goal is not to assign blame but to acknowledge past injustices and work towards healing and reconciliation.

The commission also discussed the importance of centering the voices and perspectives of communities most affected by historical injustices. Commissioner Jamal Abdullah suggested establishing partnerships with local organizations serving communities of color to ensure their involvement in the reparations process.

A vote was taken on the establishment of a research team, which passed 8-2 with Commissioners James Johnson and Sarah Lee dissenting due to concerns about resource allocation and potential duplication of effort.
