#### Meeting Info
* **Type:** Housing and Redevelopment Authority (HRA) meeting
* **Purpose:** Review of tentative developer request for Jackson Street Apartments at Zero Jackson St. development proposal, discussion on downtown 2050 plan and comprehensive plan update, and review of HRA quarter one budget and budget to actual report.
* **Date/Time:** April 22, 2026, 2:00 PM
* **Attendees:** Commissioners Johnson, Kim, Bowie, Noecker, Deputy Director Green, Director McMahon, Tia Anderson (project manager in economic development), David Vang (DC Commercial executive), JB Vang team members

#### Summary
**Jackson Street Apartments Proposal**

* DC Commercial and JB Vang are seeking tentative developer status for a 1.73-acre site at Zero Jackson St.
* The project would be a 60-80 unit affordable housing development with 12 supportive housing units.
* The proposal includes outdoor amenity space, surface and structured parking, and pedestrian access on Jackson Street.

**Commissioner Questions and Comments**

* Commissioner Bowie asked about DC Commercial's status as an emerging developer and their partnership with JB Vang.
* Commissioner Noecker inquired about the mentorship role of JB Vang's team and the alignment of the site with their team's goals.

**Downtown 2050 Plan and Comprehensive Plan Update**

* The comprehensive plan update, called Imagine 2050, is a 10-year cycle required by state law through Met Council.
* The downtown 2050 plan is an update to the existing downtown development strategy adopted in 2003.
* A consultant team will be working with city staff on the project, focusing on core substantive components and policy framework.

**HRA Quarter One Budget and Budget to Actual Report**

* PED's budget to actual report is a new process that began this year, aiming to provide more detailed information on spending.
* The report shows some variances in programs such as supportive housing, fix-up programs, and rental rehabilitation.
* Commissioner Noecker suggested adding a projection or column to the report showing when revenues are expected to be received.
* Deputy Director Green noted that PED's revenue sources, including the HRA's housing trust fund, are depleting.
