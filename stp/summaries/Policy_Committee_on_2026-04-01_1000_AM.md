#### Meeting Info
**Meeting Type:** Policy Committee
**Purpose:** Discussion on Signage Ordinance and potential zoning study
**Date/Time:** 2026-04-01, 10:00 AM
**Speakers:** Joe Spencer (St. Paul Downtown Alliance), JP (St. Paul Area Chamber), Lynn Pingal (Business Review Council Chair), Angie Wiese (Director of Safety Inspections), Tony Johnson and Jervais from Planning and Economic Development

#### Summary

The Policy Committee met to discuss the signage ordinance and potential zoning study. Joe Spencer and JP from the St. Paul Downtown Alliance and Chamber presented on the issue, highlighting that the current signage code is creating real barriers to business vitality and downtown vitality. They proposed reforming the sign code to make it easier for businesses to obtain permits and install signs.

Lynn Pingal, Chair of the Business Review Council, presented a cultural destination area study that highlighted the importance of signage and wayfinding in St. Paul. She noted that the study found common issues with signage barriers and wayfinding problems across various districts.

Angie Wiese from the Department of Safety Inspections presented on the current sign code, which is 40 pages long and regulates every aspect of signs, including size, location, and type. She noted that this creates a significant burden for businesses and proposed simplifying the code to make it easier to follow.

Tony Johnson and Jervais from Planning and Economic Development presented on the zoning study scope, which will cover citywide changes to the sign code. They noted that they are trying to consolidate many of the regulations and simplify the process for obtaining permits.

The committee members were generally supportive of the proposals and discussed potential next steps. Some concerns raised included ensuring that any changes would benefit all business corridors in the city, not just downtown. The committee agreed to move forward with initiating a zoning study and making subsequent amendments to the zoning code.

Additionally, Chris Hicks from Protect Borrowers presented on employer-driven debt, specifically "traps" or training repayment agreements, which can be used by employers to coerce employees into staying at their jobs. He noted that these agreements are often hidden in employment contracts and can result in significant financial penalties for employees who leave their jobs. The committee members were concerned about this issue and discussed potential ordinances to address it.

The meeting was adjourned after over two hours of discussion, with the next steps being the initiation of a zoning study and subsequent amendments to the zoning code.
