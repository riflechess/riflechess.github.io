#### Meeting Info
Meeting Type: Housing & Redevelopment Authority (HRA) Public Meeting
Date/Time: February 25, 2026, 2:00 PM
Location: Saint Paul City Hall
Purpose: To discuss the Emergency Rental Assistance Program (ERA), Down Payment Assistance program, and related budget allocations.

#### Summary

The HRA meeting focused on two main topics: the Emergency Rental Assistance Program (ERA) and the Down Payment Assistance program. The ERA staff report provided updates on the 2025 program results, including a grant amount of $2,500 for families earning up to 80% AMI. Staff proposed increasing this amount to $3,500 in 2026.

The Down Payment Assistance program update discussed the program's goals and successes in 2025, with an average purchase price of $265,000 and a household size increase to 2.45 people. Commissioner Noecker suggested revisiting the verification process for renters who were displaced by I-94 construction. Commissioner Bowie expressed concern about the lack of data on racial demographics and urged further exploration.

Regarding staffing, the ERA program aims to hire two additional staff members using funds from the housing trust fund. This will help address the current staffing capacity issue. The meeting also discussed potential collaboration with Ramsey County and other partners.

The resolution to amend the Housing Trust Fund Project Budget was approved, allocating $926,222 for staffing in the Department of Planning and Economic Development (PED) for ERA citywide. This will provide a total of $2.5 million available for emergency rent assistance in 2026.

Key decisions:

* Increase grant amount for ERA from $2,500 to $3,500 in 2026
* Allocate $926,222 from the housing trust fund for staffing in PED for ERA citywide
* Approve the resolution to amend the Housing Trust Fund Project Budget

Notable discussion points:

* Concerns about racial demographics data and the need for more disaggregated data
* Suggestions for revisiting the verification process for renters who were displaced by I-94 construction
* Urgency around staffing capacity and potential collaboration with Ramsey County and other partners
