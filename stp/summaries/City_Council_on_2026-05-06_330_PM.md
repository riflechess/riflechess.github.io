#### Meeting Info
Meeting Type: City Council Meeting
Purpose: To discuss various resolutions and ordinances, including public hearings on sound-level variances and bond sales.
Date/Time: 2026-05-06, 3:30 PM
Location: City Hall, Saint Paul

#### Summary
The meeting began with the introduction of new members to the Labor Standards Advisory Committee, Michelle Collins, Ollie Grotsky, and Sir Waugh. The council approved their appointments with a unanimous vote.

The next item on the agenda was Resolution 26-619, which urged European financial institutions to divest from companies that enable US Department of Homeland Security and Immigration and Customs Enforcement. However, Councilor Kim withdrew this item due to a clerical error.

Public hearings were held for various ordinances, including:

* Ordinance 26-25: Amending Chapter 66 of the Legislative Code to correct a Scrivener's error related to green roof space.
* Ordinance 26-26: Updating Chapter 310 of the Legislative Code pertaining to uniform license procedures.
* Resolution Public Hearing 26-103: Authorizing issuance of and awarding sale of general obligation various purpose bonds, series 2026B.

All public hearings resulted in unanimous approval with no opposition.

The council also approved resolutions for bond sales, including:

* Series 2026B: A tax levy to support the issuance of $245 million in general obligation bonds.
* Series 2026C: A tax levy to support the issuance of $50 million in general obligation bonds.

Other notable items included:

* Resolution Public Hearing 26-109: Approving an interim use permit for a temporary cellular telephone antenna on a 76-foot monopole tower at 732 Central Avenue West.
* Multiple resolutions approving sound-level variances for various events and venues, including El Burrito Mercado, Ordway Center, University of St. Thomas Campus Life, Dual Citizen Bruins, Skinner's Pub, Wabasha Brewing, and Ramsey County Regional Railroad Authority.

Councilor Bowie expressed support for the resolution on the cellular antenna, stating that it was necessary to ensure connectivity in the area. She also encouraged residents to have conversations with the leadership at Pilgrim Baptist Church regarding concerns about the tower.

The meeting concluded with a discussion of good news from around the city, including an upcoming community conversation on Tenant Protections and a spring tea event at Hallicube Brown Community Center.
