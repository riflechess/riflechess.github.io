#### Meeting Info
**Type:** Policy Committee Meeting
**Purpose:** Discussion of Council compensation structure, Cultural Destination Area studies, and Arts & Culture initiatives.
**Date/Time:** June 24, 2026, at 10:00 AM

#### Summary
The meeting covered three primary policy areas focused on city governance, economic development through culture, and specialized roles for the arts.

**Council Member Compensation and Status**
The committee discussed a multi-year effort to transition City Council positions from "part-time" to "full-time" status in both the city charter and administrative code.
*   **Rationale:** The current "part-time" designation is seen as a barrier to attracting diverse candidates, particularly those who cannot hold secondary jobs or manage their own businesses while serving. 
*   **Proposal:** Transitioning to a professionalized compensation model similar to Minneapolis. This includes removing the specific part-time/full-time distinction and moving toward a system where pay is set via comparative analysis of peer cities rather than fixed percentages.
*   **Leadership Perks:** There is strong support for "supplemental pay" for those in leadership roles (Council President and Vice President) to reflect the increased administrative responsibilities such positions entail.

**Cultural Destination Areas (CDA) Study**
The committee reviewed a study aimed at identifying and developing specific zones as engines for economic growth.
*   **Scope:** The report identified several districts—including Downtown, Rondo, Little Africa, and others—that could be leveraged to boost tourism and trade.
*   **Business Support:** A major focus was on how these areas can better support immigrant-owned businesses, which represent a significant portion of St. Paul's commerce but often face gaps in accessing city resources.
*   **Proposed Budget & Actions:** The study suggests a $756,000 investment to implement:
    *   A "Buy Local St. Paul" program (starting with a sticker program for local goods).
    *   Improved wayfinding and signage systems.
    *   Codification of the cultural zones in city law to ensure they remain protected as priority areas.

**Arts & Culture Strategy**
Following the CDA study, the committee heard proposals regarding the role of arts in the city's infrastructure.
*   **Director Role:** A case was made for reinstating an Arts and Culture Director or a dedicated liaison to coordinate between various departments (Public Works, Parks & Rec, etc.) and the tourism sector.
*   **Funding Models:** Discussions were held regarding the use of "public art programs" that can be integrated into capital budgets (proposing 1-1.5% allocations) to fund public infrastructure while enhancing the city's aesthetic value.
*   **Community Engagement:** The goal is to move toward a model where art isn't just an addition, but part of the "sense of place" that makes St. Paul a distinct destination for residents and visitors alike.
